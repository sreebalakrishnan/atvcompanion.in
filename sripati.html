Sripati Paddhati

A quadrant-based house system from 11th-century India. The why behind it — geometry, latitude, the M.C. — and the how, walked through with Vikram's own example.

§ One

Origin · Sripati, 11th century

Sripati was an Indian astronomer and mathematician (c. 1019–1066 CE), best known for his treatise Siddhānta Śekhara. The house-division method that bears his name — Sripati Paddhati (literally "Sripati's procedure") — is a quadrant-based system: it divides the celestial space into four quadrants using the Ascendant and the Medium Coeli, then sub-divides each quadrant into three houses.

It sits alongside Whole Sign (Parashara, Jaimini, Mantreshwara) and Equal House as one of three house systems Vikram covers in the class. Each answers a slightly different question about where the planet sits in the sky.

§ Two

Three systems, one chart

A planet doesn't move when you switch house systems — but the house number assigned to it can. Each system makes a different choice about where one house ends and the next begins.

Whole Sign · Rāśi
One sign = one house

The rising sign (Lagna) is the entire 1st House. The next sign is the entire 2nd House, and so on. Prescribed by Parāśara, Jaimini, Mantreshwara — the system Vikram uses by default.

Equal House
Each house = exactly 30°

The rising degree is the midpoint of the 1st House. House extends 15° either side of the Ascendant. Then 30° increments forward. Used in some classical texts and in some modern Western systems.

Sripati Paddhati · this page
Quadrants from Asc + M.C., trisected

Uses the actual angular distance between Ascendant and Medium Coeli (10th cusp) — divides each quadrant into three. Latitude-aware: at the equator it collapses toward Equal House; at higher latitudes the houses become unequal.

Placidus · not used in class
Time-based quadrant division

Western tradition. Listed here for completeness — Vikram does not use Placidus in the class. Mentioned only when students ask why Western charts look different.

§ Three

Why Sripati exists — the geometry

At the equator, the M.C. (Medium Coeli — the highest point of the ecliptic at the moment of birth) coincides with the Zenith, the point directly overhead. As you move north or south, those two points separate. Sripati Paddhati is the procedure that accommodates this separation and still produces meaningful house cusps.

"At the equator, the Medium Coeli and the Zenith are the same. But if I go north — or south — it moves away from the zenith. That's what brings us to Sripati Paddhati." — Vikram, Session 37 · Sripathi Paddhathi & Houses · 1 March 2026

The anatomy

Four anchor points define the quadrants:

  • Ascendant (Asc) — eastern horizon. Cusp of 1st House.
  • Medium Coeli (M.C.) — highest point of the ecliptic. Cusp of 10th House.
  • Descendant (Desc) — opposite the Ascendant, +180°. Cusp of 7th House.
  • Imum Coeli (I.C.) — opposite the M.C., +180°. Cusp of 4th House.

Cusps of the remaining houses (2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9, 11, 12) are computed by trisecting each quadrant's arc along the ecliptic. The house extents are then the midpoints between consecutive cusps.

Note: in Sripati Paddhati, a "cusp" is the middle of a house, not its boundary. This is the conceptual flip from Whole Sign — it takes a moment to settle.

§ Four

Vikram's worked example

From deck 66. Step by step. Real numbers.

The given chart

22° Aquarius rising · 10th cusp at 25° Scorpio

Convert to absolute longitude (0° Aries = 0°, going clockwise on the wheel):

Step 1 · Cusp of 1st House (Asc)
22° Aquarius = 300° + 22° = 322°
Step 2 · Cusp of 10th House (M.C.)
25° Scorpio = 210° + 25° = 235°
Step 3 · Quadrant size (Asc → M.C.)
Going clockwise from M.C. up to Asc: 322° − 235° = 87°. Trisect into three houses (12, 11, 10):
Each house = 87° / 3 = 29°
Step 4 · Cusp of 12th House
322° − 29° = 293° → 23° Capricorn
Step 5 · Cusp of 11th House
293° − 29° = 264° → 24° Sagittarius
Step 6 · House extents (start/end points)
Cusps are midpoints; the boundaries are the midpoints between consecutive cusps:
End of 12H / start of 1H = (322° + 293°) / 2 = 307.5°
End of 11H / start of 12H = (293° + 264°) / 2 = 278.5°
Step 7 · The other two quadrants
By symmetry: I.C. (4th cusp) = M.C. + 180° = 55°. Desc (7th cusp) = Asc + 180° = 142°. The remaining cusps (2, 3, 5, 6, 8, 9) are derived the same way — trisect each quadrant.

The math is mechanical once you see the rhythm. The conceptual leap is just "cusps are midpoints, not edges."

§ Five

When does Vikram use which?

In the class, the Whole Sign system is the default. Sripati Paddhati gets pulled in when the question is specifically about geographic latitude or when students want to compare with Western charts they've seen.

For most chart reading — the work in Read · beta, the prompts in Reflect, the cross-references in Learning Companion — Whole Sign is what's running underneath. Sripati lives here as foundational understanding, not as the working surface.

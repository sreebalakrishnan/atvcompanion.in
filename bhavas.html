The matchbox · why this exercise

"I want to give you all an activity. What I want you to do is to get 12 matchbooks. Probably the matches. Keep the boxes, label the boxes one to twelve. And as we go into the significations of each house, I want you to look around you and see if there's anything around you on your desk, in your kitchen, wherever, that can fit into a matchbox. That symbolises that house."

"You need to connect — all these houses are all around you wherever you go. So start looking at using the houses as a filter to view the world. You'll improve your vocabulary a lot. Keep looking for items that you can slip into this matchbox."

The pedagogical stance: "There are some words here that I don't resonate with. It does not apply to my lifestyle. So I leave them. I work with what is applicable to my life. See what you connect with."

— Vikram Devatha · Sessions 36–38, leading into the Ether retreat

Twelve houses · South Indian layout

This is a Bhava chart, not a Rashi chart — the twelve cells are houses (1 to 12), not signs. The arrangement is the South Indian convention used elsewhere in this app: H1 at the second column of the top row, going clockwise.

Tap any chip to mark it resonant · long-press (or shift-click) to strike out. Add your own items to the matchbox below each drawer.

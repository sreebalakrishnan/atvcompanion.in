what we're building

A learning companion for Vikram's cohort

The Astrology 101 Companion App is a quiet, opinionated companion to Vikram Devatha's Astrology 101 class at All Things Vedic. It's a place to revisit what was taught — element by element, planet by planet — and to interpret your own chart in the way Vikram has been teaching us.

It's not a chart reader. It's not predictive. It deliberately doesn't use a language model to interpret your chart. Vikram's "intuition, not memory" is the through-line — and intuition isn't something we want a model to dress up as personal insight.

The longer arc — and we're saying this carefully — is to build something the cohort can lean on instead of legacy software like Parashara Light. Not by matching its scope, but by raising the standard on what a learning companion can be: high tech, high touch. Built with the cohort, for the cohort, alongside Vikram's class.

principles, not a roadmap

How we decide what to build

We don't have a feature backlog. We have five principles. Anything that doesn't fit one of them, we don't build.

  1. 1. Start small. Solve our own pain first. If it's not real for the team, it won't be real for anyone else.
  2. 2. Pull, never push. We don't pitch features at the cohort. We ask what's hurting, what's missing, what's calling.
  3. 3. No LLM-driven chart interpretation. High tech, high touch as the discipline. Compute we'll happily lean on; meaning is for the human.
  4. 4. Build something we love using. If we don't reach for it ourselves, we redesign or remove it.
  5. 5. The point is the community. The app is the medium for staying in touch. The medium serves the relationship, not the other way around.
how we work

KISS, lean, fail fast — and real users

The way we build matches the way we decide. Light tools, short loops, real cohort members in the room from the start.

  1. · KISS — keep it simple. The smallest thing that solves the pain. No clever architecture for clever's sake.
  2. · Lean and agile. Tiny releases, frequent course-corrections. We've shipped 200+ versions in six months — most of them under 50 lines of code each.
  3. · Fail fast, learn fast. If a v0 doesn't land in a week of cohort use, we tweak it; if it doesn't land in a month, we remove it.
  4. · Build, launch, test, iterate. In that order. The launch isn't the end — it's the beginning of the test.
  5. · Real users, real feedback. No focus groups, no synthetic personas. Cohort members on WhatsApp, in class, in the page. The page is the conversation.

what we've heard

The pull-based pattern, in practice

Almost every section of the app exists because a cohort member said something specific, and we built a small thing to answer it. A few examples — all real, all attributed:

  • Vikram said the cohort needs a place to reflect — not just learn. → Reflect, the contemplative companion to every section.
  • Arpitha was getting overwhelmed and needed to know where she was in the syllabus — what had landed, what hadn't. → Where am I, a self-assessment across all 58 topics in seven sections.
  • Sudha said she'd like to remember what we discussed in the Elements class. → Elements, six tiles per element with the cohort's own contributions.
  • Sathvika said she learns through visuals and spatial layouts, not text. → Interactive · beta, the planetary cabinet as a tappable stage.
  • Vikram shared the All Things Vedic style guidelines and asked us to align. → a site-wide brand pass, plus a Legacy ↔ ATV brand toggle on Interpret so we could compare both visual languages side by side.
  • Sree wanted a deeper read on Mercury — beyond the line in Vikram's deck. → Mercury Spectrum, the Buddh ↔ Buddha continuum as a tool for seeing yourself.
  • Sree wanted to track his Jupiter — false expectation versus divine grace, after Vikram's auction-game session. → Jupiter Tracker, eight slider pairs and three reflection prompts.
  • Anmol reached out after Vikram showed the page in class — pointed at farfaraway.co (clean, modular, statements-not-predictions, in the Vikram style) and kundligpt.com as references. → conversation in flight on which module he'd love to take a v0 of.

Notice the shape. Each one starts with a person and a pain, never a feature spec. That's deliberate. We'd rather build six small things people asked for than one large thing nobody did.

what we'd love help with

Soft asks, in order of need

This list is genuinely soft. None of it is urgent. All of it is welcome whenever it calls you.

  • Proof-readers, module by module. The KB is dense; Vikram's exact phrasing matters; one set of careful eyes per section is gold. — now
  • Manual + automated testers across form factors. The page works on iOS Safari, Android Chrome, desktop — but every cohort member has a different device and a different rough edge waiting to be found. — now
  • Pedagogy thinking. Sanchi joins this work next week. Anyone with a teaching or learning-design background who wants to think alongside her would be welcome. — from next week
  • Module owners — someone who feels strongly enough about one section (a planet, a house, a yoga, an avatar) to bring it to life and rally the first three students to test it. — ongoing
  • Marketing, finance, support — eventually, when we have something the cohort lives in and want to share more widely. Not now. Filed for later. — later
how to engage

If something's calling you

Tell me which section of the app calls you. Tell me which module you think we can improve. You should feel a strong calling, and be ready to build a v0 — then bring the first cohort members to test it.

Not "here's the backlog, pick one" — instead, "what's your pain, what have you noticed in others, what can we solve together?"

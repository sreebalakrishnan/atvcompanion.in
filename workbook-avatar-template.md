# Companion Workbook · Avatar Module Template

A shared shape for the workbook team (Marie · Sudha · Mannat · Spandana with Vikram · Megha · Neha) to draft each avatar against, and for the integration into the Astrology 101 Companion App.

**Drafted as the worked example: Matsya** — already live in the app. Use it as the reference.

## Authoring rhythm

1. The workbook team drafts a section in this shape (one `.docx` per avatar, or all in one).
2. Send to Sree.
3. Integration into `quick-reference.html` happens within ~24–48 hours.
4. The drawer for that avatar carries the same depth as Matsya's.

When the docx uses **blue text** to flag content for integration, that's honoured — anything blue lands first.

## The shape — nine sections per avatar

### 1. Story
A 5–10-step retelling, short enough to be remembered. Optionally a "Remember By" mnemonic per step.
*Matsya example:* Demon steals Vedas → ordinary king praying → tiny fish appears → fish outgrows every container → "build a boat in 7 days" → flood comes → 40-day journey to Himalayas → knowledge restored → first humans · the Manu race begins.

### 2. Planetary link
Which graha does this avatar carry? One sentence in Vikram's voice if available.
*Matsya example:* Matsya → Ketu (south node of the moon). "Parashara says Matsya Avatar is associated with Ketu."

### 3. Quiz
2–4 multiple-choice questions, each with 4 options + the correct index + a short hint that grounds the answer in Vikram's class material.
*Matsya example:* "Which planet did Parashara associate with Matsya?" → Ketu · "Ketu is which node of the moon?" → South.

### 4. Check-in
A short framing line that invites the reader into reflection + 1–2 prompts they sit with *before* they read the module. Keep the framing as an instruction (what to do), not a re-telling of the myth — the myth already lives in the Story section.
*Matsya example:* "Before you read or hear the story, take a little time to reflect on these questions." → "Where do you feel 'too big' for your current container?"

### 5. Reflection clusters
Each cluster is `theme + Vikram quote + 2 prompts`. As many as the team has — Matsya has eleven.
*Matsya example cluster:* On listening — "We allow the higher to speak through anything…" → "What 'small fish' is speaking to you right now that you have been ignoring because it seems too small, too strange, or illogical?"

### 6. Chart application
The "Find your X" exercise — what to look for in your chart for this avatar's planet.
Authored at three tiers when possible:
- **Beginner** — no chart needed, conceptual
- **Intermediate** — sign + house of the planet
- **Advanced** — nakshatra, dispositor, transit layers
*Matsya example:* "Find your flood" — Ketu's sign + house + nakshatra · what must be saved.

### 7. Yoga pose
A pose that embodies the avatar / planet. Title, brief instruction, and modifications by masculine/feminine sign or active/surrendered orientation if relevant.
*Matsya example:* The Fish pose — backbend with the crown of the head to the floor. Active version (yang sign) vs. bolster-supported surrender version (yin sign).

### 8. Pranayama
A breath practice that names what the avatar is teaching the body.
*Matsya example:* 4-count inhale, gradually extending the exhale — letting go.

### 9. Check-out
A bookend at the end of the module — re-visits the check-in prompt with one or two follow-up questions for after the reading.
*Matsya example:* "Look at that restrictive 'container' you identified in the Check-in. If you stop trying to 'see' the path forward, what does your inner Matsya tell you to save?"

### Optional · Listening / ritual
A practice that crosses into sense-and-intuition territory. Indigenous rituals, mushroom listening, threshold-crossing — only when relevant. Matsya has one. Most avatars probably don't need one.

## Where each section lives in the app

`quick-reference.html` → Avatars section → tap an avatar tile → drawer opens.

Inside the drawer, in order:
1. **Check-in** card (when authored)
2. Story (2 paragraphs at most)
3. **From-the-class** expandable — Vikram's actual telling, scene by scene, from the session transcript
4. **Sit with this** expandable — eleven reflection clusters
5. **Practice with this** expandable — chart application + yoga + pranayama + ritual
6. **Check-out** card (when authored)

The cross-avatar **Table 3** (Avatar / Planet / Brain region / Trauma / Life expression) sits above the avatars as a separate view.

The **Quiz** is part of the Avatars-section quiz at the bottom of the section, not inside individual drawers — keeps the quiz UX consistent across sections.

## Sharing a specific avatar

Each drawer has a `🔗 Copy link` chip in the title — copies a URL like:

```
https://sreebalakrishnan.in/astrology101/quick-reference.html#avatar-matsya
```

Anyone you send that to lands directly inside the open drawer. Use this when sharing with Vikram, the workbook team, or the cohort to point at one avatar specifically.

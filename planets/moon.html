Astronomy — the Moon's physical nature and movement
▾

Vikram begins every planet with its astronomy. The physical facts of how the Moon moves and behaves in the sky are the foundation for its astrological meaning. Each fact maps to a quality.

Fact → Meaning

Astronomical factWhat it means in Vedic astrology
Much smaller than Earth, but impacts the tidesEmotions
Illuminates the nightThe mind
Periodically waxes and wanesHabits
Extreme temperaturesEmotional instability
Looks soft and beautifulBeauty, nourishment
Closest to Earth of all the NavagrahasRuler of the senses
The tiniest body amongst the Navagrahas, yet appears the same size as the SunAre we making something insignificant very important?

Movement and cycles

  • Sidereal month: 27–28 days — one complete orbit around Earth relative to the fixed stars.
  • Synodic month: 29.5 days — Full Moon to Full Moon; the cycle we feel.
  • Per sign: approximately 2 days and 6 hours in each zodiac sign.
  • Per Nakshatra: one Nakshatra per day — the Moon's daily address in the sky.

The fortnights — Paksha

  • Shukla Paksha — the bright fortnight, waxing from New Moon to Full Moon.
  • Purnima — Full Moon. The Moon at peak reflected light.
  • Krishna Paksha — the dark fortnight, waning from Full Moon back to New Moon.
  • Amavasya — New Moon. No reflected light. A time of inner turning.

Mythology — Krishna and the Moon
▾

Vikram links the Moon to Krishna — the eighth avatar of Vishnu. The mythology is not decorative; it is a map of the inner world, showing what the Moon actually governs in a human life.

Moon's themes through Krishna

  • Nurtures and protects
  • Equanimous — steady in the midst of chaos
  • Unconditional love
  • Charioteer of the soul — the wise inner guide

The philosophical reading

The Mahabharata maps onto the inner human: the chariot is the body (your Ascendant). Krishna, the charioteer, guides the five Pandava brothers — who represent the five sense organs — against a hundred challenges (the Kauravas).

  • Yudhishtira (son of Yama) · Bhima (son of Vayu) · Arjuna (son of Indra) · Nakula & Sahadeva (sons of the Ashwini Kumars) — the five sense organs
  • Draupadi — the Kundalini; she calls on Krishna when the senses cannot protect her
  • Hanuman — ambassador of Rama (the Soul), sits atop the chariot throughout the war

Forms of devotion

  • The Gopis — different kinds of devotion: playful, joyous, surrendered
  • Radha — the highest form of love; the soul's desire to merge with Source, inseparable from Krishna
  • Rukmini — love and devotion coupled with righteousness and duty

Moon's traits and classical significations
▾

Parashara on the Moon

"Moon represents the mind. Chandra is very windy and phlegmatic. She is learned and has a round body. She has auspicious looks and sweet speech, is fickle-minded and very lustful."

From Uttara Kalamrita — selected significations

  • Mind, mental disposition, virtue or vice
  • Intelligence, heart, happiness
  • Sleep, nourishment, eating
  • Mother, woman, beauty, face
  • Flowers, good perfume
  • Silver, pearls, soft and silk garments
  • Water — wells, tanks, ponds
  • Strength at night · facing westward
  • Fame · travel · Brahmin
  • Worshipping Goddess Gauri
  • Fish and other water creatures

One technical note

The Moon aspects only the seventh house from wherever it sits. A direct gaze across the chart — self to other. (BPHS 26:2-5)

Three special Moon yogas (BPHS 24)

  • Anapha — planets (except Sun) in the 12th from Moon → free from disease, virtuous, fame, comforts, dignity
  • Sunapha — planets (except Sun) in the 2nd from Moon → wealthy, intelligent, self-earned prosperity, good speech
  • Kemadruma — no planets before or after the Moon → poor, miserable, lacks support

Moon through the twelve signs
▾

The Moon moves through all twelve signs in a lunar month. Where it was at the moment of your birth shapes the emotional tone of your inner world.

Two signs to know: the Moon is exalted in Taurus (most comfortable) and debilitated in Scorpio (most challenged).

Moon through the twelve houses
▾

The sign tells you the quality of your emotional world. The house tells you where that quality shows up most in your life.

Moon's natural house: The Moon is the natural Karaka (significator) of the 4th house — Sukha Bhava, the house of happiness. The 4th house signifies: mother, home, land, vehicles, the heart, and things connected with water. (BPHS · Deck 67)

Moon interpretation — reading your chart
▾

Vikram teaches interpretation through two anchors. Together they help you write a line about your Moon placement that feels true — without reducing it to a prediction.

Anchor 1 — Parts of Speech

Every chart placement can be read as a sentence. Each grammatical role maps to a chart factor:

GrammarChart factorFor the Moon
NounPlanetMoon — emotions, mind, nourishment, habits
VerbDuality (Yin / Yang)Inward (receptive) or outward (expressive)?
AdverbModality (Cardinal / Fixed / Mutable)Initiates, sustains, or adapts emotionally?
AdjectiveElement (Fire / Earth / Air / Water)The feeling quality: fiery, grounded, airy, watery?
Area of lifeHouseWhere emotional needs show up most
Worked example — Vikram's own (Deck 73)
"Reflecting on my emotions supports me and my family."
Moon in Taurus · 2nd house — Moon (emotions) · Taurus (receptive, fixed, earth) · 2H (family, speech, wealth)

Anchor 2 — The Intention

The same placement can be offered in four ways. Vikram's year-1 students use three:

  • To open a conversation: "Have you noticed how your emotional needs show up in your home life?"
  • To invite action: "Can you try attending to your emotional needs first thing each morning, and notice what shifts?"
  • As a remedy: "Nourishing your inner world before you nourish others may help."
  • To predict the future — the one framing to avoid in year 1.

Integration — remedies and Moon-aligned practices
▾

The Moon governs the mind — and through the mind, sleep, digestion, and emotional rhythm. Remedies for the Moon are not dramatic; they are small, consistent acts of self-nourishment.

Sleep guidance — from Deck 73

The Moon rules the fourth house of rest. Disturbances to sleep — mental activity, stress, screens, emotional turbulence — are Moon disturbances. What Vikram's deck offers:

  • Avoid caffeine and heavy meals in the evening
  • Cut mental activity and reduce screens before sleep
  • A consistent sleep and wake schedule — the body loves rhythm
  • Box breathing (inhale 4, hold 4, exhale 4, hold 4) as you lie down
  • Viparita Karani (legs-up-the-wall) — settling for the nervous system
  • Look at the sun in the morning — helps regulate the cycle
  • A brief wind-down ritual that signals the day is done
Integration reflection — from avatars/08-krishna.md (Deck 49)

"How easy is it for you to 'feel' what others may be going through? Is this a strength or a weakness?"

"Think about a time when you found answers, or hope, or some insight during a challenging time. Can you recreate it the next time you face a challenge?"

## The placement

> **Sun** in the **9th sign · Sagittarius**, sitting in the **11th house**.
>
> *(Implied Lagna: Aquarius — since Whole Sign places the 11th house at the 11th sign from Lagna, and Sagittarius is the 11th from Aquarius.)*

## Building blocks — one sentence per block

Following Vikram's golden rule (*one building block per sentence — never combine two*):

- **Sagittarius · Yang** — Ambitious *(the duality / direction of expression)*
- **Sagittarius · Mutable** — Spontaneous *(the modality / how the action happens)*
- **Sagittarius · Fire** — Transformative *(the element / quality of the action)*
- **Sun in the 11th house** — Recognition *(the area of life)*

> **First-pass synthesis:** *"I am ambitious and adaptable in my creativity."*

## Two synthesis sentences — same fact, two voicings

From the same five-cell row in the table, the framework yields more than one valid sentence. Word choice carries the learner's voice:

> *"The self expresses outwardly, spontaneously and transformatively, in the area of recognition and community."*
>
> or
>
> *"The self shines transformatively by spontaneously seeking recognition and vision."*

The factors are identical. The phrasing is the learner's. This is exactly the room Vikram's principle protects — *"don't let the computer take away the human."*

---

## What this example demonstrates

- **The framework yields a valid line** even when the learner is reading their own chart for the first time.
- **The framework yields multiple valid lines** — there's room for voice within the structure.
- **The "I am" form lands naturally** — it isn't formulaic, it's a path from grammar to selfhood.
- **The Intention is implicit and right** — no prediction here, just self-recognition. *"I am ambitious and adaptable in my creativity."* opens a conversation with the self, exactly the framing Vikram teaches.

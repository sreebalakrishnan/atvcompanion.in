# 5. Vamana (Vamana)

**Order:** 5 of 10

## At a glance
The fifth avatar of Vishnu — a small Brahmin boy who asks the asura king Bali for three paces of land and grows to span the cosmos. Vikram links Vamana to Jupiter: expansiveness, wisdom and law, the guardian whose true power is veiled in humility.

## Story
Bali, the virtuous asura king (and grandson of Prahlada), grows powerful enough to threaten the heavens. During Bali's yagna, Vishnu appears as Vamana, a small Brahmin boy, and asks for three paces of land. Bali's preceptor Shukracharya warns him, but Bali grants the wish. Vamana then expands to cosmic scale: with two steps he covers earth and heavens. For the third step, Bali offers his own head. Vamana presses Bali down to Patal Loka (the netherworld), grants him dominion there, and promises that Bali will become Indra in a future age. Vamana does not destroy Bali; he blesses him.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
However powerful one becomes, there is always something vaster than the self — true growth lies in bowing to the infinite. Vishnu's choice to come as a dwarf teaches that the divine doesn't always appear grandly; like the pillar in the Narasimha story, this story asks us to stay open to the sacred in small everyday objects and people. Bali's offer of his head as the third step is the highest form of charity — not giving possessions, but giving up identity and pride. Dharma is restored not by fighting the wicked but by restoring balance with compassion. In surrendering, Bali attains immortality; spiritual immortality comes from letting go, not holding on.

## Astronomical / cosmological reading
The universe expanded from a tiny singularity after the Big Bang, just as the small boy becomes large enough to cross all worlds — scale and complexity emerging from something very small. Vamana's three steps may represent space, time and ego; if we want to control and dominate nature we must surrender to the Divine. The grandest power can be humbled by something very small. Vamana could also represent gravity — an invisible, underestimated force that governs everything. Scholars also read the three steps as the three positions of the Sun: rising, peak, and setting.

- "Vamana's cosmic expansion is like unchecked ambition. Have your desires ever outgrown what you could hold? What did you learn from that?"

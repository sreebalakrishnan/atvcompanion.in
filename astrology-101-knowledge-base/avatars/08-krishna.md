# 8. Krishna (Krishna)

**Order:** 8 of 10
**Form:** Cowherd, prince, charioteer / Krishna
**Planet (Graha):** Moon (Chandra)
**Yuga:** Dwapura Yuga
**Source decks:** Deck #49 (Krishna), Deck #44 (Cosmology of Time), Deck #55 (All the Avatars)

## At a glance
The eighth avatar of Vishnu — Krishna, born to Vasudeva and Devaki in Kamsa's prison, raised among the cowherds, charioteer of Arjuna in the Mahabharata. Vikram links Krishna to the Moon: nurturing and protecting, equanimity, unconditional love, the wise strategist who illuminates the dark.

## Story
Krishna's life arc, as captured by the deck's slide titles: Dramila; Kamsa's tyranny; the imprisonment of Vasudeva and Devaki; Yogamaya; survival from Putana and Trinavarta; Kaliya Mardan (subduing the serpent); Pulastya Muni; defying Indra by lifting Govardhan hill; Rasa Lila with the Gopis; Radha and Krishna; the killing of Kamsa; the founding of Dwaraka; marriage to Rukmini; relationship with Draupadi; the revelation of Vishwarupam (the universal form). The Mahabharata is dated to 3102 BC in the deck.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
The deck maps the Mahabharata onto the inner human:
- **The Gopis** — different kinds of devotion: playful, joyous.
- **Radha** — the highest form of love; the soul's desire to merge with Source, inseparable from Krishna.
- **Rukmini** — love and devotion coupled with righteousness and duty.
- **The Pandavas** — the five sense organs: Yudhishtira (son of Yama), Bhima (son of Vayu), Arjuna (son of Indra), Nakula and Sahadeva (sons of the Ashwini Kumars).
- **Draupadi** — the Kundalini, abused by life's challenges; she calls out to Krishna because the sense organs cannot protect her.
- **Krishna, the charioteer** — guides the five sense organs against a hundred challenges.
- **The chariot** — the body. Hanuman, the ambassador of the Soul, sits atop the chariot throughout the war.

**Rama vs Krishna** (the deck's explicit contrast):
- Both serve Dharma; both are selfless, humble, equanimous.
- Rama is the ideal human; Krishna is the wise strategist.
- Rama prioritises Dharma; Krishna goes after Adharma.
- Rama follows the rules even when it hurts him; Krishna bends the rules to protect dharma.
- Rama uses conventional decisions; Krishna strategises with the end in mind.
- Rama renounces; Krishna embraces power, love, politics, war.
- Rama's life is sacrifice and renunciation; Krishna's is engagement with the world.
- Rama is about living rightly; Krishna is about living wisely.

## Self-reflection prompts
From Deck #49 — astrological reflections framed as Krishna as the Moon:

**Nurtures and protects.** "Which is your earliest memory of being nurtured and protected? How has that affected you? How do you nurture and protect others?"

**Detached and equanimous.** "When was the last time you were triggered? What could you have done to stay balanced? What can help you stay equanimous in future?"

**Capacity to guide others.** "Think about a time when you guided or supported someone going through a difficult situation. How can this capacity be strengthened in you?"

**Compassion and empathy.** "How easy is it for you to 'feel' what others may be going through? Is this a strength or a weakness?"

**Unconditional love and devotion.** "What does 'unconditional love' mean to you? In what form or manner have you experienced this in your life? What was the impact on you and others?"

**Illuminates the dark.** "Think about a time when you found answers, or hope or some insights during a challenging time. Can you recreate it the next time to face a challenge in life?"

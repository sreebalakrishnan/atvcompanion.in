## At a glance
The ninth avatar of Vishnu — Shakyamuni Buddha, the awakened one. Vikram links Buddha to Mercury: intelligence, communication, the Middle Path. The deck delivers his life events, the Four Noble Truths and the Eightfold Path, and an etymological reading of "Buddha".

## Story
The Buddha's life arc, as captured by the deck's slide titles: birth at Lumbini to King Suddhodana and Queen Maya Devi; Samvega in Ashada (the awakening of urgency); studies under the teachers Alada Kalama and Udaka Ramaputta; Sujatha's offering of milk-rice; Mulacinda (the naga king); the Descent from Tavatimsa Heaven; Devadutta's elephant; the Jewelled Walkway; the Twin Miracle.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
The deck offers an etymological reading of the avatar's name and title:
- **Budh** — to wake up, be awake, observe, heed, attend, learn, become aware of, to know; also a title for those who have attained.
- **Buddhi** — that which helps us discern truth from falsehood.
- **Tatha gata** — one who has gone.
- **Tatha aagata** — one who has come.
- **Tatha agata** — one who has not gone.
- **Tathagata** — one who is beyond coming and going.

**The teaching — Four Noble Truths:**
1. Life is full of suffering.
2. Attachment is the root cause of suffering.
3. Let go of attachment, you will let go of suffering.
4. To let go of attachment, follow the Eightfold Noble Path.

**The Eightfold Noble Path**, grouped into three:
- **Wisdom (Prajna)** — Right View (understanding the truths of life); Right Intention (free of ill-will and harmful desires).
- **Ethics (Sila)** — Right Speech (truthful, kind, helpful communication); Right Action (moral and peaceful behaviour); Right Livelihood (earning a living without harming others).
- **Discipline (Samadhi)** — Right Effort (cultivating positive mental states); Right Mindfulness (awareness of body, feelings and thoughts); Right Concentration (deep focus through meditation).

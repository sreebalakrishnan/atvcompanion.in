## At a glance
The seventh avatar of Vishnu — Rama, prince of Ayodhya, hero of the Ramayana. Vikram links Rama to the Sun: dharma and righteousness, steadfast loyalty, the just and noble ruler. The deck reads the Ramayana as a soul-mind-ego story mapped onto every character.

## Story
Narada speaks with Brahma; the Krauncha episode introduces the avatar. In the Kingdom of Kosala, King Dasharatha has four sons — Rama, Lakshmana, Bharata and Shatrugna — born to his queens. As a youth, Rama protects sage Vishvamitra's yagna and lifts (and breaks) Shiva's bow to win Sita. The plot of Kaikeyi and Manthara sends Rama, Sita and Lakshmana into 14 years of exile (Vanvasa). In the forest, Surpanakha's overture leads to Sita's abduction by Ravana. The Pushpak Vimana carries Sita away; Jatayu tries to stop it. Rama allies with the Vanaras and Hanuman, builds Ram Sethu (Adam's Bridge) to Lanka, fights Ravana in Ashok Vatika, and rescues Sita. Sita undergoes Agni Pariksha at Divirumpola. Later episodes touch Sahasra Ravana and Kali Mata, and the twin sons Luv and Kush. Rama returns to Ayodhya — celebrated as Diwali — and reigns as Ram Rajya.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
The deck reads the Ramayana as a map of the soul:
- **Dasharatha** ("rider of ten chariots") — the human ruled by ten senses (five of perception, five of action).
- **Kausalya** ("skillfulness") — the one who acts skillfully and wisely, giving birth to Rama.
- **Ayodhya** ("a-yuddha", without battle) — the inner state free of conflict, in which the light of the soul shines naturally.
- **Rama** — the inner light / Paramatma; "Ra" (light) + "Ma" (within); the pure consciousness inside each of us.
- **Lakshmana** — ever-present awareness / Ananta Sesha; awareness inseparable from the soul, following Rama as awareness follows being.
- **Sita** — mind / Prakriti / Jivatma; the manifest nature, the individual self born of the Earth, that gets separated from the inner light.
- **Bharata** — devotion to light and knowledge.
- **Ravana** — the ten-headed ego, with its desire, pride, attachment.
- **Abduction of Sita** — Prakriti (the mind) kidnapped by ego; the soul loses connection with its pure nature.
- **Hanuman** — Prana / life-force; breath, knowledge and devotion that reunite the soul with the mind.

## Astronomical / cosmological reading
The Sun journeys through light and darkness. Rama is from the Solar dynasty. His exile to the southern forests indicates the Sun's southern journey (Dakshinayana) — light fading. Sita's abduction is energy and vitality falling, darkness growing. Hanuman, the breath, bridges soul and nature, restoring balance. Rama's victory over Ravana marks the Sun's gradual return north (Uttarayana), before Ram Rajya. His return to Ayodhya, celebrated as Diwali, symbolises the restoration of dharma both outside and within.

## Self-reflection prompts
From Deck #41 — astrological reflections framed as Rama as the Sun:

**Just and noble ruler.** "Rama ruled Ayodhya with fairness and wisdom, earning the love and respect of his people. His reign, Ram Rajya, is the symbol of just and noble leadership. When was the last time you led a team or project? Did you make decisions that were fair and respected by everyone? What qualities do you admire in good leaders? Did you express any of those qualities in your own leadership?"

**Righteousness.** "Though he was the rightful heir, Rama accepted his exile to the forest without complaint, honouring his father's word and staying true to dharma. He gave up his comfort for a higher purpose. Have you ever given up comfort to do what is right? What values do you refuse to compromise, even when it's hard?"

**Courage and valour.** "Rama showed great bravery in his battle against Ravana to rescue Sita. His courage came from love, duty, and purpose. What or who would you stand up for, no matter what? What gives you strength to face challenges?"

## At a glance
The sixth avatar of Vishnu — "Rama with the axe", a Brahmin who acts as a Kshatriya. Born to Jamadagni and Renuka at the hermitage of Janapav, he wields the axe to reset corrupt rule. Vikram links Parashurama to Venus: devotion, inner fulfilment, and the perfection of knowledge that must be lived.

## Story
Parashuram is born to the sage Jamadagni and Renuka at Jamadagni's hermitage in Janapav. The hermitage holds Surabhi, the divine wish-fulfilling cow. The Kshatriya king Kartavirya Arjuna covets Surabhi and his army takes her by force. Parashuram retrieves her and, in the cycles of conflict that follow, repeatedly culls corrupt Kshatriyas — his battles famously located at Samantha Panchaka. The deck also references an episode involving Renuka and the Gandharvas. Parashuram is depicted as both Brahmin scholar and warrior — knowledge integrated with action.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
Parashuram embodies inner and outer mastery: self-discipline and clarity (Brahmin) integrated with action and skill (Kshatriya). True evolution requires both — knowledge and application, academics and practice. Dharma is not fixed but contextual: a Brahmin acting as a Kshatriya is dharmic when time, place and inner clarity demand it. Knowledge must be embodied — lived through action, tested and refined. This is jnana evolving into karma and then into viveka (discernment). His culling of corrupt kings is the painful clearing needed for new birth — destruction aligned with dharma, necessary for evolution.

## Astronomical / cosmological reading
Parashuram's repeated culling of corrupt Kshatriyas mirrors the cyclical nature of the universe — stars are born, shine, collapse or explode, and are reborn. Systems are reset when they become imbalanced. Parashuram is a union of opposites — Brahmin (knowledge) and Kshatriya (action) — paralleling wave and particle, matter and antimatter, entropy and order. The universe seems held together by opposing forces in balance. Surabhi can be seen as an invisible field of abundance that, under the right conditions, gives rise to particles and worlds — abundance arising from tuning into what is already present.

## At a glance
The fourth avatar of Vishnu — half-man, half-lion — who emerges from a pillar at twilight to slay Hiranyakashipu and protect his devotee Prahlada. Vikram links Narasimha to Mars: aggressive energy directed by dharma, standing up against injustice, reality that breaks binary logic.

## Story
Hiranyakashipu, brother of Hiranyaksha (slain by Varaha), seeks revenge. He performs severe penance and obtains a boon from Brahma making him near-invincible: he cannot be killed inside or outside, day or night, on the ground or in the sky, by weapons or by hands, by human or animal, by living or non-living, by deity or demon. His son Prahlada, however, is an unwavering devotee of Vishnu. To bypass every clause of the boon, Vishnu incarnates as Narasimha — neither fully man nor animal — emerges from a pillar (neither inside nor outside) at twilight (neither day nor night), and kills Hiranyakashipu on his thigh (neither ground nor sky) using his claws (neither weapon nor hand). After the act, Narasimha rages on; the forms Veerabhadra and Sharaba are invoked to pacify him.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
Prahlada stays calm and centered while everything around him is in chaos — the still inner observer, the steadiness when everything else is falling. Hiranyakashipu represents the ego that tries to dominate nature, convinced it is above the gods; a mere stone pillar humbles him. Nature cannot be controlled, and if we forget that, we are corrected by forces greater than us. Narasimha — neither man nor animal, appearing at neither day nor night — breaks all binary logic, showing that reality can hold contradictory truths and is stranger than we think.

## Astronomical / cosmological reading
Narasimha emerges from a pillar — not the sky, not the earth, but something in between. The deck reads this as a symbol for hidden dimensions or forces in the universe: wormholes, quantum-scale strangeness, dormant power waiting to reveal itself in unexpected places. Narasimha is super-violent yet protects the innocent — like supernovae and galactic collisions that destroy and yet create and protect what life needs. Black holes consume everything, yet shape galaxies. Destruction is not always negative.

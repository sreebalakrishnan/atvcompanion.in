# 3. Varaha (Varaha)

**Order:** 3 of 10

## At a glance
The third avatar of Vishnu — a boar who dives into the cosmic ocean to rescue Bhudevi (Mother Earth) from the asura Hiranyaksha. Vikram links Varaha to Rahu: the urge to go to the depth, the courage to face the unconscious, the rescue of Dharma from darkness.

## Story
The four Kumaras — Sanaka, Sanandana, Sanatana, Sanat Kumara — curse Jaya and Vijaya, Vishnu's gatekeepers, who reincarnate as the asura brothers Hiranyaksha and Hiranyakashipu. Hiranyaksha kidnaps Bhudevi and drags her into the cosmic ocean. Vishnu incarnates as Varaha, a boar, dives into the ocean, slays Hiranyaksha, and lifts the Earth back up on his tusks. Place names associated with the lore include Trimbak and the Shipra River.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
Hiranyaksha is ego, greed and obsession with power. Bhudevi symbolises Truth and Dharma — hijacked by ego and dragged into the ocean of tamas (darkness, inertia, confusion). Dharma waits to be rescued through deep, inner effort. Varaha is the unexpected, primal drive of the Divine to go where the ordinary mind cannot — diving into the unconscious unafraid, retrieving what is sacred, bringing it back to awareness. This is the essence of sadhana. The tusks are viveka (discernment) — sharp, strong, clear, piercing through illusion.

## Astronomical / cosmological reading
The deck offers Varaha as a black-hole / white-hole metaphor. Bhudevi sinking into the cosmic ocean parallels creation being pulled into a black hole — a chaotic, insatiable consumer of light and order. Varaha diving in and lifting creation back up suggests that black holes may not truly destroy creation; matter may be hidden but recoverable. Varaha may then be a "white hole" — the opposite of a black hole, where matter and information re-emerge — symbolising that everything can return even from total collapse.

## Self-reflection prompts
From Deck #19:
- "Bhudevi was taken to the depths of the ocean. Similarly, sometimes parts of us get buried deep under layers of emotion, and it takes courage to bring them back to light. What part of you feels like it has sunk into chaos or confusion? How can you go deep to retrieve it?"
- "Varaha emerged in a wild, unexpected form - half-boar, half-god. He challenged conventional ideas of 'God' and showed that power can arise from unlikely shapes. Have you ever been misunderstood or treated like an outsider because of a path you chose?"
- "Hiranyaksha believed he had won, but it was an illusion that could not last. When have you mistaken illusion for truth? Or chased something that turned out to be empty in the end?"
- "Hiranyaksha's drive for power made him sink the Earth itself, showing how desires can lead to destruction rather than fulfilment. Do you have any desires that is making you sink to the bottom?"
- "The cosmic ocean may be symbolic of the mind, so large that it seems to swallow the whole Earth. Are you amplifying anything out of proportion?"

## At a glance
The first avatar of Vishnu — a fish who guides Satyavrata through the great flood, recovers the stolen Vedas, and ushers in the next age. Vikram links Matsya to Ketu: the energy of higher guidance, detachment, and total reset.

## Story
A great flood approaches. Vishnu takes the form of Matsya, a fish, and tows Satyavrata's boat to safety through the deluge. Matsya also recovers the Vedas, which had been stolen by the demon Hayagriva, restoring sacred knowledge. Satyavrata, having released his grip on his kingdom and people, is transformed into Vaivasvata Manu — the progenitor of the next age — alongside Shraddha. Matsya is the first divine descent, and the deck notes its echo in 40+ flood myths across world cultures.

## Symbolism — philosophical reading
Matsya represents the higher force that guides us when ordinary reality dissolves. Satyavrata's surrender — letting go of kingdom, people, stability — is the Ketu posture: detachment as the precondition for transformation. The flood is simultaneously an ending and a beginning; the fish towing a boat through chaos is faith in something beyond logic. Matsya recovering the Vedas suggests that essential knowledge is never truly lost — it is preserved and returned through divine intervention when the world resets.

## Self-reflection prompts
From Deck #7, framed as Matsya as Ketu (South Node of the Moon):
- "Matsya guides Satyavrata through a flood, to safety, and to wisdom. Can we allow a Higher Force to lead us in times of difficulty? How can we do that?"
- "Satyavrata had to let go of everything - his kingdom, his people and what he considered to be stable. Can we stay detached? Maybe we need to lose something to find something new?"
- "Matsya taught Vedic wisdom to the sages and to Satyavrata. Is the universe trying to teach us something, when we believe everything around us is falling apart?"
- "Satyavrata becomes Vaivasvata Manu, a turning point for the world. So the flood is an end to something, and a beginning of something new. A kind of a reset. How can we welcome a full reset in our lives?"
- "The flood and storm and idea of a fish towing a boat is a total disconnection from logic and reality. Can we have faith in the illogical?"
- "Satyavrata doesn't cling to power, people or to his kingdom. What are we clinging on to? How can we release our grip on it?"

# Deck 19: Varaha Avatar

**Source file:** `19. Varaha Avatar.txt`
**Type:** Avatar / Mythology

## Topic
The Varaha (boar) avatar of Vishnu — the rescue of Bhudevi (Mother Earth) from the asura Hiranyaksha who dragged her into the cosmic ocean. Deck explores the story astronomically, philosophically, and as material for self-reflection.

## Key concepts
- Backstory: the four Kumaras (Sanaka, Sanandana, Sanatana, Sanat Kumara) curse Jaya and Vijaya (Vishnu's gatekeepers), who reincarnate as the asura brothers Hiranyaksha and Hiranyakashipu.
- Hiranyaksha kidnaps Bhudevi and sinks her into the cosmic ocean.
- Vishnu incarnates as Varaha (boar), dives in, slays Hiranyaksha, and lifts the Earth back up on His tusks.
- Astronomical lens: Bhudevi sinking into the cosmic ocean as a metaphor for matter falling into a black hole; Varaha as a possible "white hole" — a return of matter and information from collapse.
- Philosophical lens: Hiranyaksha as ego/greed; Bhudevi as Truth/Dharma hijacked by ego and dragged into tamas (darkness, inertia); Varaha as the primal divine drive that dives into the unconscious to rescue Dharma; tusks as viveka (discernment) cutting through illusion.
- Self-reflection prompts: parts of self buried under emotion; being misunderstood for unconventional paths; mistaking illusion for truth; desires that pull one down; amplifying things out of proportion.

# Deck 7: Matsya Avatar

**Source file:** `7. Matsya Avatar.txt`
**Type:** Mythology / Avatar (linked to Ketu)

## Topic
First of Vishnu's 10 avatars — Matsya (the fish). Story of the great flood, rescue of Satyavrata/Vaivasvata Manu, and astrological reflections mapping Matsya to Ketu (south node of the Moon).

## Key concepts
- Matsya rescues Satyavrata (later Vaivasvata Manu) from the great flood, towing his boat to safety
- Recovers the stolen Vedas from the demon Hayagriva
- Vaigai river setting; Matsya Purana as source
- Vaivasvata Manu and Shraddha as progenitors of the next age
- Flood myths exist in 40+ cultures globally — deck lists parallels: Mesopotamian (Utnapishtim, Epic of Gilgamesh), Abrahamic (Noah's Ark), Greek (Deucalion & Pyrrha), Chinese (Gun-Yu), Mesoamerican (Maya, Aztec), North American (Cree, Choctaw, Hopi), South American (Inca, Tupi), African (Kwaya, Mande, Yoruba), Oceania (Aboriginal, Maori, Samoan), SE Asia (Indonesian, Filipino)
- Astrological mapping: Matsya = Ketu (South Node of the Moon)

## Reflection themes (Matsya as Ketu)
1. Journey to higher knowledge — allowing a higher force to lead in difficulty
2. Detachment and loss — Satyavrata letting go of kingdom, people, stability
3. Insight and knowledge — universe teaching when everything seems to fall apart
4. Huge transformation — flood as ending + reset; Satyavrata becomes Manu
5. Disconnection from logic — faith in the illogical (a fish towing a boat through a flood)
6. Independence — releasing grip on power, people, kingdom

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Matsya — fish (avatar)
- Avatar — divine descent
- Manu / Manvantara — progenitor of an age
- Ketu — south lunar node

## Names / references mentioned
- Hayagriva, Satyavrata / Vaivasvata Manu, Shraddha, Maharishi Parashara
- Vaigai river
- Matsya Purana
- 10+ flood-myth cultures listed

## Topic
Repeats the "Bar in Heaven" story (Steve Jobs, Napoleon, Mandela meet Varaha as bartender) and then provides the worked answer: classifying each character by Duality (Yang/Yin) and Modality (Cardinal/Fixed/Mutable) based on observed behaviour.

## Key concepts
- Steve Jobs = Yang + Mutable. Yang because he is energetic, outward-facing, restless, initiates conversation. Mutable because he orders both drinks, shifts metaphors rapidly (market -> UX -> OS), and adapts.
- Napoleon Bonaparte = Yang + Fixed. Yang because decisive, assertive, makes bold first orders. Fixed because love is conquest, "one bold entrance can define everything", resistant to change once committed.
- Nelson Mandela = Yin + Cardinal. Yin because he listens, responds thoughtfully, seeks harmony over dominance. Cardinal because he leads with vision/principles - sets tone, frames love as a journey, initiates cautions - without aggressive Yang push.
- Demonstrates that Cardinal/Fixed/Mutable behaviour can be inferred from how a person moves through a situation, independent of Yang/Yin polarity.

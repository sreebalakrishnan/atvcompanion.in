# Deck 58: Case Study - The Lizard Family

**Source file:** `58. Case Study - The Lizard Family.pdf`
**Type:** Case Study

## Topic
A whimsical scenario casting the learner as a "Parent Lizard" who must respond to skeptical objections about astrology raised by Grandfather Lizard and Teenage Lizard at breakfast. Sets the discussion up but stops before resolving it.

## Key concepts
- Setting: the Lizard family at breakfast in the month of Ashada, eating fried mosquitoes, dragonflies, and squished-ant juice.
- Skeptical objections raised:
  1. "Astrologers consider only 12 stars out of millions" - implying astrology arbitrarily picks signs.
  2. "Astrologers treat Sun and Moon as planets" - whereas the Sun is a star and the Moon is a satellite.
  3. Implicit: "If it cannot be proved by science, then..." - questioning whether astrology is a science.
- Prompt: "How would you, a Parent Lizard, an avid student of Vedic Astrology, respond?"

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Ashada — Hindu calendar month (mentioned in setting).

## Names / references mentioned
- (Fictional characters: Grandfather Lizard, Teenage Lizard, Parent Lizard.)

## Notes
Deck 58 is the **setup-only** version of the case study - the extracted text ends at the prompt without giving the answer. Deck 60 contains the same setup plus the resolution (Stars vs Signs, Planet vs Graha, astrology-as-language). Used to spark discussion in class.

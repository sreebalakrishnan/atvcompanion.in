# Deck 55: All the Avatars

**Source file:** `55. All the Avatars.pdf`
**Type:** Mythology / Avatars recap

## Topic
A consolidating recap that maps each of the ten avatars (Dasavatara) to a Graha and to one of the four Yugas, summarising the astrological qualities of each and the civilizational/evolutionary problem that each Yuga represents.

## Key concepts
- Avatar-to-Graha mapping with key qualities:
  - Matsya — Ketu — Protection and foresight; transformation; preservation of knowledge; divine intervention to restore balance.
  - Kurma — Saturn — Emotional churning; stability; patience and perseverance; Mount Mandara.
  - Varaha — Rahu — Going to the depth; protector of the weak; tilling the land.
  - Narasimha — Mars — Aggressive energy; witnessed injustice; stood up for someone or something.
  - Vamana — Jupiter — Expansiveness; wisdom, law, justice; guardian.
  - Parashuram — Venus — Devotion; inner fulfillment; perfection of knowledge.
  - Rama — Sun — Dharma and righteousness; steadfastness and loyalty; bravery and courage.
  - Krishna — Moon — Nurtures and protects; equanimous; unconditional love.
  - Buddha — Mercury — Intelligence; communication; Middle Path.
  - (10th avatar, Kalki, is not explicitly named in extracted text but Kali Yuga is mapped.)
- Yuga framework (problem each Yuga represents):
  - Satya Yuga — Problems about survival; non-human/semi-human; good and evil in different worlds; violence; vertebrates evolving to mammals.
  - Treta Yuga — Problems about duty and honour; Dharma and hierarchy; fully human; good and evil in same world; mammals/primates to humans.
  - Dwapura Yuga — Problems about greed and moral confusion; good and evil in same family; strategic; no rigid rules; context is key; dharma is debated.
  - Kali Yuga — Problems with superficial nature, low inner values; good and evil in same person; no heavy metaphysics, no misuse of ritual or violence; pre-frontal cortex (latest in human evolution).

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Yuga — cosmic age (Satya, Treta, Dwapura, Kali).
- Dasavatara (implicit) — the ten avatars.

## Names / references mentioned
- Avatars: Matsya, Kurma, Varaha, Narasimha, Vamana, Parashuram, Rama, Krishna, Buddha.
- Mount Mandara (used in Samudra Manthan).
- Grahas: Ketu, Saturn, Rahu, Mars, Jupiter, Venus, Sun, Moon, Mercury.

Recap deck cross-referencing avatar mythology with the planetary cabinet and the Yuga framework as a kind of evolutionary/civilizational arc. Kalki is not explicitly mentioned in the extracted text - likely shown visually only. Useful as the spine of the entire mythology block.

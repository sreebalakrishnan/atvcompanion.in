# Deck 27: RA & Declination

**Source file:** `27. RA & Declination.txt`
**Type:** Astronomy fundamentals

## Topic
Introduces the equatorial coordinate system: Right Ascension (RA) and Declination — the celestial analogue of longitude and latitude, fixed relative to the stars.

## Key concepts
- Recap: degrees-minutes-seconds (360 deg, 60', 60"); Rigveda 1.164.48 reference.
- Recap: Earth's grid, axial tilt (~23.5 deg), revolution, ecliptic, celestial equator.
- Right Ascension (RA): celestial longitude measured in hours, not degrees. 24 hours = 360 deg, so 1 hour = 15 deg, 2 hours = 30 deg. Measured eastward along the celestial equator from the vernal equinox (0h).
- Declination: celestial latitude measured in degrees north (+) or south (-) of the celestial equator; ranges from -90 to +90.
- Reference points marked: 23.5 deg (Tropic of Cancer), -23.5 deg (Tropic of Capricorn), and the celestial poles at +/-90.
- Worked example homework: coordinates of Sun, Moon, Mars on 26-Feb-2022 at 3pm in Kiev, Ukraine — table given:
  - Sun: Az 168, Alt 30, RA 22h 36m, Dec -08
  - Moon: Az 221, Alt 01, RA 18h 43m, Dec -27
  - Mars: Az 211, Alt 11, RA 19h 44m, Dec -22

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Right Ascension — celestial longitude (in hours)
- Declination — celestial latitude (in degrees)

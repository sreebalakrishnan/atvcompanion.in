# Deck 24: Celestial Equator

**Source file:** `24. Celestial Equator.txt`
**Type:** Astronomy fundamentals

## Topic
Introduces angular measurement (degrees-minutes-seconds), Earth's grid (latitude/longitude), axial tilt, the ecliptic, and the celestial equator as the projection of Earth's equator onto the sky.

## Key concepts
- A circle has 360 degrees; each degree has 60 minutes (arcminutes); each arcminute has 60 seconds (arcseconds). Distinct from time hh:mm:ss.
- Rigveda 1.164.48 quoted: "Twelve spokes, one wheel, navels three. ... three hundred and sixty like pegs" — Vedic resonance with 360-degree division.
- Earth's grid: latitude (parallel to equator) and longitude (pole-to-pole meridians).
- Earth's axial tilt (~23.5 deg) drives seasons via revolution around the Sun.
- The Ecliptic: the apparent annual path of the Sun against the background stars (the plane of Earth's orbit).
- Sunrise position varies across the year (visualized via 12-month sunrise sequence Jan-Dec).
- The Celestial Equator: projection of Earth's equator onto the celestial sphere; tilted ~23.5 deg relative to the ecliptic.
- Celestial longitude marked in degrees around a 360-deg ring (0, 30, 60, ..., 330).

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Ecliptic — apparent path of the Sun
- Celestial Equator — projection of Earth's equator on the sky
- Axial tilt / obliquity (~23.5 deg)

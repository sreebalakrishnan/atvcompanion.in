# Deck 67: The Houses (ver 2)

**Source file:** `67. The Houses ver 2.pdf`
**Type:** Astrology fundamentals / Houses

## Topic
Second iteration of "The Houses" deck. Where deck 64 stopped at 2H, this version covers all 12 Houses — adding BPHS Sanskrit shlokas (with transliteration and translation) for each House and listing significations from Uttara Kalamrita, Sarvartha Chintamani, and Hora Sara.

## Key concepts
- 12 Matchboxes exercise repeated
- Each House now has: BPHS Sanskrit shloka, transliteration, English translation, plus multi-source significations
- House summary table with karakas and groupings:
  - 1H Tanu — Kendra, Trikona, Dharma; Sun
  - 2H Dhana — Maraka, Artha, Panapara; Jupiter
  - 3H Sahaja — Apoklima, Upachaya, Kama; Mars
  - 4H Sukha — Kendra, Moksha; Moon
  - 5H Putra — Panapara, Trikona, Dharma; Jupiter
  - 6H Rog — Apoklima, Upachaya, Dushtana, Artha; Mars and Saturn
  - 7H Kalatra — Kendra, Kama, Maraka; Venus and Jupiter
  - 8H Aayur — Dushtana, Panapara, Moksha; Saturn
  - 9H Bhagya — Dharma, Trikona, Apoklima; Jupiter
  - 10H Karma — Kendra, Artha, Upachaya; Saturn, Sun, Mercury, Jupiter
  - 11H Labha — Panapara, Upachaya, Kama; Jupiter
  - 12H Vyaya — Apoklima, Dushtana, Moksha; Saturn
- Closing reference to Brihat Jataka (Varahamihira) lists three alternative naming schemes for each House (body/family/siblings/...; ability/inherence/valor/...)

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- All 12 House Sanskrit names (Tanu, Dhana, Sahaja, Sukha, Putra, Ripu/Rog, Yuvati/Kalatra, Randhra/Aayur, Dharma/Bhagya, Karma, Labha, Vyaya)
- Upachaya — growth houses (3, 6, 10, 11)
- Dushtana — malefic houses (6, 8, 12)

## Names / references mentioned
- Parashara — Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra (Chapter 11 throughout)
- Kalidasa — Uttara Kalamrita
- Varahamihira — Brihat Jataka
- Prithuyasas — Hora Sara
- Venkata Sharma — Sarvartha Chintamani

## Notes
Major expansion over deck 64. The Sanskrit shlokas appear in Devanagari with corrupted glyphs in extraction (some IAST diacritics broken) but transliterations and translations are clean. Deck 64 was effectively a partial draft; this is the first complete version.

# Deck 49: Krishna

**Source file:** `49. Krishna.pdf`
**Type:** Mythology / Avatar (8th)

## Topic
The Krishna Avatar - 8th of the dasavatara, associated with the Moon. Covers his life events, philosophical interpretation of the Mahabharata characters, astrological qualities of Krishna, and a comparison with Rama.

## Key concepts
- Life-event slide titles: Dramila, Kamsa, Vasudeva and Devaki, Yogamaya, Putana, Trinavarta, Kaliya Mardan, Pulastya Muni, Defying Indra, Govardhan, Rasa Lila, Radha and Krishna, Killing of Kamsa, Dwaraka, Rukmini, Draupadi, Vishwarupam.
- Mahabharata dated 3102 BC.
- Philosophical readings:
  - Gopis = different kinds of devotion (playful, joyous).
  - Radha = highest form of love; the soul's desire to merge with Source.
  - Rukmini = love and devotion coupled with righteousness and duty.
  - Pandavas = the 5 sense organs (Yudhishtira/Yama, Bhima/Vayu, Arjuna/Indra, Nakula and Sahadeva/Ashwini Kumars).
  - Draupadi = the Kundalini abused by life's challenges, calling out to Krishna.
  - Krishna = the charioteer guiding the senses against 100 challenges.
  - Chariot = the body; Hanuman atop = ambassador of the Soul.
- Astrological themes (self-reflection prompts):
  - Nurtures and protects.
  - Detached and equanimous.
  - Capacity to guide others.
  - Compassion and empathy.
  - Unconditional love and devotion.
  - Illuminates the dark.
- Rama vs Krishna contrast:
  - Both serve Dharma; both selfless, humble, equanimous.
  - Rama = ideal human; Krishna = wise strategist.
  - Rama prioritises Dharma; Krishna goes after Adharma.
  - Rama follows the rules even when costly; Krishna bends rules to protect dharma.
  - Rama = conventional decisions; Krishna = end-in-mind strategy.
  - Rama renounces; Krishna embraces power, love, politics, war.
  - Rama = sacrifice and renunciation; Krishna = engagement with the world.
  - Rama = living rightly; Krishna = living wisely.

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Vishwarupam — Krishna's universal/cosmic form.
- Rasa Lila — Krishna's divine dance with the gopis.
- Govardhan — the hill lifted while defying Indra.
- Kaliya Mardan — subduing the serpent Kaliya.
- Kundalini — referenced via Draupadi metaphor.
- Dharma / Adharma — righteousness / its violation.

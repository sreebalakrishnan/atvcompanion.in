# Deck 15: Duality and Modality

**Source file:** `15. Duality and Modality.txt`
**Type:** Astrology fundamentals (sign classification)

## Topic
Full version of the sign-classification lesson. Duality (yin/yang, masculine/feminine) AND Modality (cardinal/fixed/mutable, Sanskrit: chara/sthira/dviswabhava). Anchored in Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra Ch 4 Verse 5.

## Key concepts

**Duality (same as Deck 14):**
- 12 signs split into Yin and Yang
- 12 reflective prompts on rest vs push, vulnerability vs competition, etc.

**Yin/Yang remedies (full table):**
- Exercise: Yin = tai chi, walking, swimming; Yang = running, Surya Namaskars
- Food: Yin = cooling, soft teas, fragrances; Yang = hot, spicy
- Pranayama: Yin = Brahmari, Ujjayi (slow & long); Yang = Kapalabhati, Bhastrika (rapid & active)
- Meditation: Yin = breathing meditations, guided visualizations; Yang = pranic flow, faster scans
- Routine: Yin = bedtime schedule; Yang = active morning routine
- Mantras: Yin = Devi mantras; Yang = Shiv Tandav

**BPHS 4.5 verse:** "carasthiradvisvabhāvāḥ krūrākrūrāu narastriyau / pittānilatridhātvaikyaśleṣmikāśca kriyādayaḥ" — the Signs are movable, fixed, or dual; harsh or gentle; masculine or feminine; linked to pitta, vata, tridhatu, or kapha in nature and activities.

**Three classifications from BPHS 4.5:**
1. **Modality:** Chara (movable/cardinal), Sthira (fixed), Dviswabhava (dual/mutable)
2. **Duality:** Krura (cruel/harsh) vs Akrura (gentle); Nara (masculine) vs Stri (feminine)
3. **Dosha:** Pitta (bile/fire), Anila/Vata (wind), Tridhatu Aikya (three together), Shleshmika/Kapha (phlegm)

**Modality + Seasons:**
- Cardinal/Chara — sign at the start of each season (Aries-spring, Cancer-summer, Libra-autumn, Capricorn-winter); 14 Apr, 15 May, 17 Aug, 17 Sep etc. tropical-zodiac dates listed
- Fixed/Sthira — middle of each season (Taurus, Leo, Scorpio, Aquarius)
- Mutable/Dviswabhava — end of each season (Gemini, Virgo, Sagittarius, Pisces)
- Sun Sign defined as "the stars behind the Sun at the time of birth"
- Indian Makar Sankranti (14 Jan) marker shown

**Modality keywords clustering exercise:**
- Cardinal: manifest out of thin air, fresh energy, big ideas, launch new project, visionary, infectious enthusiasm, charge forward, mobiliser, ideas and visions, trend setter, restless for movement, lack energy to follow-through
- Fixed: grounding, risk-averse, doers, hardworking, dependable, patient, laser-focused, plans ahead, loyal, perseverant, identifies what needs doing, sticks to routine, slow and steady, has a sense of purpose, stubborn sometimes, dedicated
- Mutable: handles changes, flexible, non-judgemental, understands the need for change, goes with the flow, multi-tasker, mediator, freedom, collaborates

**Modality remedies:**
- Cardinal: start something new, invest in a new domain, start a new habit, experiment, initiate a new project
- Fixed: keep habits / routine, repair and restore, journal and reflect over time, grounding
- Mutable: end a habit/something, clean out the old, journal and burn to let go

**14 Modality reflection prompts:** about starting vs persevering vs completing, acting quickly vs waiting, sticking to one thing, commitment, response to challenge, flexibility vs avoidance, taking the lead, adapting to change, valuing stability vs growth, resistance to change, persistence past usefulness, motivation by beginnings vs results, losing initial-excitement interest, juggling multiple things.

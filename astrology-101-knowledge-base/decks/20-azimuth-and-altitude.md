# Deck 20: Azimuth and Altitude

**Source file:** `20. Azimuth and Altitude.txt`
**Type:** Astronomy fundamentals

## Topic
Introduces the horizontal (alt-az) coordinate system used to locate celestial objects relative to the observer's local horizon.

## Key concepts
- Azimuth: angle measured along the horizon, typically starting at North (0/360 deg) and turning clockwise — East 90, South 180, West 270.
- Altitude: angle from the horizon (0 deg) up to the zenith (90 deg).
- Each celestial object's position is given by an (azimuth, altitude) pair from a specific observer location.
- Discussion prompts: Can altitude/azimuth be negative? What are pros/cons of this coordinate system? (Implication: negative altitude = below horizon; system is observer-dependent.)

# Deck 54: Buddha Avatar

**Source file:** `54. Buddha Avatar.pdf`
**Type:** Mythology / Avatar (9th)

## Topic
The Buddha Avatar - 9th of the dasavatara, associated with Mercury. Walks through the Buddha's life events, his teaching (Four Noble Truths and Eightfold Path), philosophical etymology of "Buddha", and astrological self-reflection prompts.

## Key concepts
- Life events (slide titles): Lumbini, King Suddhodana, Queen Maya Devi, Samvega in Ashada, Alada Kalama, Udaka Ramaputta, Sujatha, Mulacinda, Descent from Tavatimsa Heaven, Devadutta's elephant, Jewelled walkway, The Twin Miracle.
- The teaching (Four Noble Truths):
  1. Life is full of suffering.
  2. Attachment is the root cause of suffering.
  3. Letting go of attachment ends suffering.
  4. Follow the Eightfold Noble Path.
- Eightfold Noble Path, grouped into three:
  - Wisdom (Prajna): Right View, Right Intention.
  - Ethics (Sila): Right Speech, Right Action, Right Livelihood.
  - Discipline (Samadhi): Right Effort, Right Mindfulness, Right Concentration.
- Philosophical/etymological notes:
  - Budh = to wake up, observe, learn, become aware, to know; also a title for the awakened.
  - Buddhi = that which discerns truth from falsehood.
  - Tathagata word-play: Tatha gata (one who has gone), Tatha aagata (one who has come), Tatha agata (one who has not gone), Tathagata (one beyond coming and going).
- Astrological themes (Mercury, self-reflection prompts):
  - Intelligence — academic understanding vs. transformative wisdom.
  - Communication — clarity and positivity vs. confusion and harm.
  - The Middle Path — balance vs. extremes.
  - Equanimity and detachment — what am I attached to.
  - Staying balanced — calm decisions vs. emotion-clouded judgment.

- Budh, Buddhi, Tathagata — etymological roots.

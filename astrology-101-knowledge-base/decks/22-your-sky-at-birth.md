# Deck 22: Your Sky at Birth

**Source file:** `22. Your Sky at Birth.txt`
**Type:** Astrology fundamentals / Lab exercise

## Topic
Hands-on exercise to physically locate where each Navagraha (planet) was in the sky at the student's moment of birth, using altitude and azimuth.

## Key concepts
- Recap: Azimuth (horizontal angle from North, clockwise) and Altitude (angle up from horizon).
- Pre-software, astrologers literally looked up at the sky to locate the navagrahas.
- Procedure:
  1. Get birth details (date, time, place).
  2. Open a sky app (e.g. Stellarium); set the birth date, time, and place.
  3. Find each Navagraha via search.
  4. Note azimuth and altitude for each graha.
  5. Step outside; use a compass app to face the right azimuth; use your hand to estimate altitude.
  6. For each planet, face that direction, be still, look, feel, notice — recognise this is where the graha was at birth.
  7. Write a few lines about how it felt; note any planet that felt stronger or more familiar.
- Goal: build a real, embodied connection with each graha; lays foundation for understanding transits mentally, spatially, and energetically.

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Navagraha — the nine grahas (planets) of Vedic astrology
- Graha — planet / celestial influence

## Names / references mentioned

# Deck 2: Hindu Vedic Scriptures

**Source file:** `2. Hindu Vedic Scriptures.txt`
**Type:** Indian Philosophy / Foundations

## Topic
Maps the landscape of Hindu/Vedic scriptural literature so students understand what "Vedic" in "Vedic Astrology" actually refers to. Walks through the categories: Shruti, Smriti, Itihasa, Purana, Agama, Darshana, with sub-divisions and example texts.

## Key concepts
- Opening provocation: SpaceX 2029 Mars mission — what would you preserve? Frames why ancient civilizations wrote scriptures.
- Classification of knowledge: Shruti (heard), Smriti (remembered), Itihasa (what happened), Purana (ancient), Agama (given), Darshana (seen) — all confirm what Shruti says.
- Four Vedas (Shruti):
  - Rig — mantras, prayers
  - Yajur — hymns offered to the Divine
  - Sama — prayers/hymns that are sung
  - Atharva — what maintains and destroys body and world
- Each Veda has four layers: Samhita (texts/rituals/hymns), Brahmana (commentary on rituals; city life), Aranyaka (esoteric meaning; forest life), Upanishad (meditation, philosophy, spiritual knowledge — usually disciple-teacher dialogues)
- Upavedas (secondary knowledge): Ayurveda (health), Dhanurveda (warfare/archery), Gandharvaveda (sound/music), Arthashastra (politics, economics, military)
- Vedangas (limbs): Shiksha (pronunciation), Vyakarana (grammar), Chhandas (rhythm/meter), Nirukta (etymology), Jyotisha (astronomy/astrology), Kalpa (ritual instructions)
- Smriti subdivisions including code of conduct in Satya Yuga, religious duty, code in Kali Yuga; Yugadharma vs Sanathan Dharma
- Itihasas: Ramayana (Treta Yuga), Mahabharata (Dwapar Yuga)
- Puranas: e.g., Vishnu Purana, Shiva Purana, Srimad Bhagavatam; Ganesha Purana, Sanat Kumara, Kapila, Surya
- Agamas: Vaishnava (worship of Vishnu, Pancharatra), Shaiva (Kashmir Shaivism), Shakta (Shakthi, Surya, Ganapati, left/right-handed Tantra)
- Darshanas (six schools): Nyaya (logic & analysis), Vaisheshika (essence of something), Sankhya (philosophy of creation), Yoga (liberation by mastering senses), Mimamsa (code of conduct in rituals), Vedanta (philosophy behind all action)
- Three Khandas: Karma Khanda (action), Upasana Khanda (worship), Gnana Khanda (knowledge)
- Four Mahavakyas (one per Veda):
  - Rig: Pragyaanam Brahma (Consciousness is God)
  - Sama: Tat Tvam Asi (You are That)
  - Yajur: Aham Brahmaasmi (I am God)
  - Atharva: Ayam Atma Brahma (This Self is God)
- 10 Mukhya (Principal) Upanishads: Isha, Kena, Katha, Prashna, Mundaka, Mandukya, Taittiriya, Aitareya, Chandogya, Brihadaranyaka — each with a one-line theme

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Shruti — heard (revealed) scripture
- Smriti — remembered (transmitted) scripture
- Itihasa — historical epic (Ramayana, Mahabharata)
- Purana — ancient lore
- Agama — sectarian worship texts
- Darshana — philosophical school (six total)
- Samhita / Brahmana / Aranyaka / Upanishad — four layers of each Veda
- Upaveda, Vedanga
- Yugadharma, Sanathan Dharma
- Karma / Upasana / Gnana Khanda
- Mahavakya — great utterance
- Pancharatra — Vaishnava Agama tradition

# Deck 36: Precession of the Equinoxes

**Source file:** `36. Precession of the Equinoxes.txt`
**Type:** Astronomy / Astrology fundamentals

## Topic
Explains the precession (wobble) of the Earth's axis, how it shifts the Vernal Equinox backwards through the zodiac over ~26,000 years, and the resulting difference between **Tropical (Western)** and **Sidereal (Vedic)** zodiacs - introducing the concept of **Ayanamsha**.

## Key concepts
- Equinoxes are the two intersection points of the Ecliptic and the Celestial Equator. Vernal Equinox = March 20 (equal day/night); Autumnal Equinox = Sept 23.
- The Vernal Equinox marks the "First Point of Aries" (0 deg).
- Due to Earth's wobble, the intersection point drifts backwards through the constellations at approximately **50 arc-seconds per year**.
- "Sun in Pisces" today (Vedic / sidereal) vs "Sun in Aries" (Western / tropical) for the same moment - the difference is precession.
- **Ayanamsha** = the offset between the tropical Vernal Equinox and the start of Ashwini Nakshatra (i.e., how far the starting point of the Zodiac has shifted backwards).
- **Age of Pisces / Age of Aquarius** terminology: when the Vernal Equinox shifts from Pisces backwards into Aquarius, it becomes the Age of Aquarius.
- Ayanamsha comparison table:
  - Tropical / Western: 0
  - Lahiri (Chitrapaksha): 50.27"/yr, coincided with Ashwini in 285 AD, current Ayanamsha 24 deg 10' 02"
  - KP New: 50.23"/yr, coincided 291 AD, 24 deg 04' 38"
  - Raman: 50.33"/yr, coincided 397 AD, 22 deg 43' 24"
- Conceptual choice highlighted: fixed reference (12 noon analogy) vs shifting reference (the moving ring) - the same question maps to Tropical vs Sidereal.
- Homework: compare planet placements in your chart under Vedic vs Tropical (Sayana) Ayanamsha.

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Ayanamsha - precessional offset between tropical and sidereal zodiacs
- Ashwini Nakshatra - starting nakshatra of the sidereal zodiac
- Sayana - Tropical (zodiac aligned to seasons/equinox)
- Nirayana (implied) - Sidereal (zodiac aligned to fixed stars)

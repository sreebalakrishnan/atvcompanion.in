**Source file:** `60. Case Study 2 - Lizard Family.pdf`

## Topic
The resolution of the Lizard Family scenario from Deck 58. Re-presents the same breakfast dialogue and then provides the three-issue framework for responding to common skeptical objections about astrology.

## Key concepts
- Same setup as Deck 58: Grandfather Lizard and Teenage Lizard objecting to astrology over breakfast in Ashada.
- The three issues to address:
  1. **Stars vs Signs** - There are millions of stars, but in astrology we consider only the stars along the ecliptic, grouped into 12. **Signs are a collection of stars**, not individual stars.
  2. **Planet vs Graha** - "Graha" means any body that moves in the sky against the backdrop of fixed stars. Since the Sun and Moon move, they are Grahas. The English word "planet" is a mistranslation.
  3. **Whether astrology is a science** - Reframe: think of astrology as a **language** rather than as science-or-not. It is a language to speak with the planets and stars.

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Graha (re-defined) — any moving body in the sky relative to fixed stars; broader than the English "planet".
- Ashada — Hindu month.

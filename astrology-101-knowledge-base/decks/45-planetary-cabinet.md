# Deck 45: Planetary Cabinet

**Source file:** `45. Planetary Cabinet.txt`
**Type:** Astrology fundamentals / Practical exercise

## Topic
Re-uses the Planetary Cabinet diagrams from deck 43 (rulership, Moola Trikona, exaltation/debilitation) and adds a **practical exercise: chain-of-Dispositors** - tracing each planet through its sign-rulers until a Final Dispositor emerges.

## Key concepts
- Same three reference wheels as deck 43:
  - **Ruler / Pati** of each sign
  - **Moola Trikona** ("Office of the Planet")
  - **Exaltation (Uccha) / Debilitation (Neecha)**
- Building Blocks of the Signs grid (Fire/Earth/Air/Water + Cardinal/Fixed/Mutable + Yang/Yin) repeated.
- **New: Dispositor exercise (the practical workflow):**
  1. Pick any graha in your chart.
  2. Determine the Sign it's placed in. Is it exalted or debilitated there?
  3. Find the **Dispositor** (the ruler/Lord of that Sign).
  4. Look at where the Dispositor itself is placed. Is it exalted or debilitated?
  5. Repeat until a planet recurs as Dispositor in the chain.
  6. The recurring planet is the **Final Dispositor** - is it exalted or debilitated?
- A blank 12-house grid (1-12) is provided for plotting the analysis.

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Dispositor - the ruler of the sign a planet currently occupies
- Final Dispositor - the planet at the terminus of the dispositor chain (where it loops back on itself)
- (Uccha / Neecha / Pati / Moola Trikona carried over from deck 43)

## Names / references mentioned
- Navagraha set: Sun, Moon, Mercury, Venus, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Rahu, Ketu

## Notes
**Difference vs deck 43:** deck 43 introduces the Planetary Cabinet *concept* (rulership / Moola Trikona / exaltation / debilitation). Deck 45 reuses the same three diagrams but is shorter on the diagram-text and adds a **"Let's analyse" practical exercise** at the end - chain through dispositors until one repeats, producing a Final Dispositor. Effectively: 43 = teaching the framework, 45 = applying it to your own chart.

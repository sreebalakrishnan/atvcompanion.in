# Deck 25: Mahalakshmi

**Source file:** `25. Mahalakshmi.txt`
**Type:** Goddess / Mythology (Tridevi / Water element)

## Topic
Introduces Mahalakshmi as one of the four great Aspects of the Mother (per Sri Aurobindo), associated with the Water element and the corresponding water signs.

## Key concepts
- Bhagavad Gita 15.1 quoted (in Sanskrit and English): the eternal ashvattha tree with roots above and branches below, whose leaves are the Vedic hymns — the knower of this tree is the knower of the Vedas.
- Element grouping of zodiac (recap): Earth, Water, Fire, Air — each with three signs.
- The four Powers of the Mother: Maheshwari, Mahakali, Mahalakshmi, Mahasaraswati (Sri Aurobindo).
- Mahalakshmi: attractive to the heart; throws the spell of the intoxicating sweetness of the Divine; grace, charm, tenderness; harmony and beauty of mind and soul; love and beauty turn life into a celestial work of art; meets devotion with the passionate attraction of the Divine.
- Group exercises (room break-outs): narrate a story for Mahalakshmi's qualities; design a fashion dress; pick an animal; plan a dinner date — all expressing Her traits.
- Self-reflection on Water qualities: feeling soft and gentle; recalling true happiness/bliss; areas of harmony; sharing kept-in feelings; calming practices for sadness/overwhelm; quality to grow this month.

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Mahalakshmi — Mother as beauty, harmony, bliss, love
- Ashvattha — the sacred fig tree (Bhagavad Gita 15.1)
- Veda-vit — knower of the Vedas
- Ananda — bliss

## Names / references mentioned
- Sri Aurobindo, "The Mother" (pp. 20, 26)
- Bhagavad Gita 15.1 (holy-bhagavad-gita.org)
- Vikram Devatha (allthingsvedic.in)

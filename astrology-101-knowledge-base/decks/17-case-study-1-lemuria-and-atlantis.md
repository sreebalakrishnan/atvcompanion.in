# Deck 17: Case Study 1 - Lemuria and Atlantis

**Source file:** `17. Case Study 1 - Lemuria and Atlantis.txt`
**Type:** Case Study (Astronomy / Time-zone math via mythology)

## Topic
A mythic narrative used as a vehicle to practise longitude-based time calculations. Brahma, Vishnu, and Shiva coordinate the destruction of two corrupt civilizations (Lemuria, Atlantis) ruled by Asuras, and the student computes local times across coordinates.

## Key concepts
- Earth rotates 360 degrees in 24 hours: 15 degrees per hour, 0.25 degrees per minute, or 1 degree per 4 minutes.
- Convert longitude differences to time differences to find local time at a remote location.
- Sample coordinates used:
  - Brahma at Pushkar (74.5 E, 26.5 N)
  - Vishnu at Tirupati (79.3 E, 13.7 N)
  - Shiva at Mt. Kailash (81.3 E, 31.0 N)
  - Lemuria near Cyprus (82.5 E, 15.0 S) [note: deck places "Lemuria near Cyprus" with eastern longitude — likely a deliberate mythic mapping]
  - Atlantis near the Canaries (33.4 E, 33.1 N)
- Worked examples convert hour differences for a "Zoom call" at 8am Brahma-time, and arrival times for Shiva's arrows at Lemuria (2pm CKT + 4.8 min) and Atlantis (6pm CKT, 47.9 deg west of Kailash).

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Asura — demonic / power-corrupt being
- CKT — "Central Kailash Time" (deck's invented reference frame, modelled on UTC/IST)

## Names / references mentioned

- Lemuria, Atlantis (lost civilizations)
- Ganesha (tusk arrow), Garuda (feather arrow)

## Notes
Pedagogical device — the legend frames a quantitative exercise on longitude-to-time conversion. Story ends with Shiva restoring balance through apparent destruction (a recurring theme: destruction as transformation).

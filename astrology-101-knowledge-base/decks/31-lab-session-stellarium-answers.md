# Deck 31: Lab Session - Stellarium (Answers)

**Source file:** `31. Lab Session - Stellarium (Answers).txt`
**Type:** Lab / Astronomy

## Topic
Answer key for the live Stellarium lab. Walks through identifying constellations, reading equatorial (RA/Dec) vs horizontal (Az/Alt) coordinates, and determining which planets are above/below the horizon and north/south of the ecliptic on a sample date.

## Key concepts
- Use the desktop Stellarium app (web version is feature-limited).
- Equatorial coordinates: Right Ascension (RA) and Declination (Dec); RA hour-angle conversion: 1h = 15 deg.
- Horizontal coordinates: Azimuth (Az) and Altitude (Alt); positive Alt = above horizon, negative Alt = below.
- Equatorial vs horizontal are two different coordinate systems with different starting points.
- The Sun lies on the Ecliptic by definition (it defines the ecliptic), so it is neither north nor south of it.
- Sign of declination indicates north (+) or south (-) of the celestial equator.
- Constellation identification on the ecliptic: Scorpio/Scorpius, Ophiuchus, Libra (straddles celestial equator), Sagittarius, Aries, Leo.
- Sample-date answers: Sun in Leo (constellation), Moon in Taurus, waning Moon (past Aquarius).
- RA-difference example: Sun RA approx 9.80h, Moon RA approx 4.45h, diff approx 5.35h ~ 80 deg.
- Two ecliptic-equator intersection points identified via nearby stars HIP 118159 (in Pisces) and HIP 58461 (in Virgo).

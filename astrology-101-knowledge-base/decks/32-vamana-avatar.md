# Deck 32: Vamana Avatar

**Source file:** `32. Vamana Avatar.txt`
**Type:** Avatar / Mythology

## Topic
Story and interpretation of Vamana, the dwarf-Brahmin avatar of Vishnu who tricks the asura king Bali into surrendering by asking for three paces of land. Covers astronomical, philosophical and self-reflective angles.

## Key concepts
- Astronomically: Vamana's growth from tiny to cosmic mirrors the Big Bang - scale and complexity emerging from a singularity.
- The three steps may represent space, time and ego; or the three positions of the Sun (rising, peak, setting).
- Vamana could also represent gravity - an invisible, underestimated force that governs everything.
- Philosophically: however powerful one becomes, something vaster always exists; true growth lies in bowing to the infinite.
- Bali offering his head as the third step is the highest form of charity - giving up identity and pride, not possessions.
- Dharma is restored not through war but through compassionate restraint; Vamana blesses Bali rather than destroying him.
- Bali gains immortality and a future role as Indra; spiritual immortality comes from letting go.
- The divine does not always appear grandly (echoes the pillar in the Narasimha story).

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Vamana - dwarf, the fifth avatar of Vishnu
- Yagna - sacrificial fire ritual
- Patal Loka / Patala - the netherworld where Bali is granted dominion
- Asura - here, virtuous king Bali

## Names / references mentioned
- Vishnu, Vamana, Bali (Mahabali), Hiranyakashyapu, Prahlada, Shukracharya (Bali's preceptor)
- Narasimha (referenced via the pillar parallel)
- Indra (Bali promised the role in a future age)

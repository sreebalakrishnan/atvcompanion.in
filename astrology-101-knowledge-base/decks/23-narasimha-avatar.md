# Deck 23: Narasimha Avatar

**Source file:** `23. Narasimha Avatar.txt`
**Type:** Avatar / Mythology

## Topic
The Narasimha (man-lion) avatar of Vishnu — slaying the asura Hiranyakashipu to protect his devotee Prahlada. Deck examines the story astronomically, philosophically, and through self-reflection.

## Key concepts
- Continuation from Varaha: Hiranyakashipu is the brother of Hiranyaksha (slain by Varaha) and seeks revenge.
- Hiranyakashipu's boon from Brahma made him near-invincible: cannot be killed inside or outside, day or night, on ground or sky, by weapon or by hand, by human or animal, by living or non-living, by deity or demon.
- Vishnu incarnates as Narasimha (half-man, half-lion) — neither fully man nor animal — and emerges from a pillar (neither inside nor outside) at twilight (neither day nor night) to bypass every clause of the boon, killing Hiranyakashipu on His thigh with His claws.
- Prahlada is the unwavering devotee whom Narasimha protects.
- Aftermath references: Veerabhadra and Sharaba (forms invoked to pacify the still-raging Narasimha).
- Astronomical lens: Narasimha emerging from a pillar evokes hidden dimensions, wormholes, quantum-scale strangeness — dormant power in unexpected places. Cosmic destruction (supernovae, galactic collisions, black holes) also creates and protects.
- Philosophical lens: Prahlada as the still inner observer amid chaos; Hiranyakashipu as ego trying to dominate nature, humbled by a stone pillar; Narasimha breaks binary logic and shows reality holds contradictory truths.
- Self-reflection prompts: aggressive energy as constructive vs destructive; standing up under opposition; reactions to injustice; tolerance for shades of grey vs binary thinking.

# Deck 46: Planetary Relationships

**Source file:** `46. Planetary Relationships.pdf`
**Type:** Astrology fundamentals

## Topic
Defines how planets (Grahas) relate to one another as Friends, Enemies, or Neutral. Covers Natural, Temporary, and Compound relationships per BPHS, with worked examples and a summary table.

## Key concepts
- Each planet has a Moola Trikona sign, used as the reference point for all relationships.
- Natural Relationships rule (BPHS 3:55):
  1. Note the Moola Trikona of Planet 1.
  2. Find the signs owned by Planet 2.
  3. If Planet 1 exalts in those signs, treat as Friend.
  4. If those signs are 2, 4, 5, 8, 9, or 12 from the Moola Trikona of Planet 1, then Friend; else Enemy.
  5. If one sign is Friend and the other is Enemy, the relationship is Neutral.
- Worked pair examples: Sun-Venus (enemy), Jupiter-Saturn (neutral/equal), Saturn-Jupiter (neutral), Sun-Moon (friend), Sun-Mars (friend), Sun-Mercury (neutral), Venus-Jupiter (neutral), Jupiter-Venus (enemy), Saturn-Venus (friend), Mars-Saturn (neutral), Moon's relationships with all other Grahas.
- Summary table of natural Friends/Enemies/Neutral for Sun, Moon, Mars, Mercury, Jupiter, Venus, Saturn, Rahu, Ketu.
- Temporary Relationships (BPHS 3:56): a planet 2, 3, 4, 10, 11, or 12 from another (by position, not rulership) is a temporary friend; otherwise enemy.
- Compound Relationships (BPHS 3:57-58): combine Natural x Temporary into Great Friend, Friend, Neutral/Equal, Enemy, Great Enemy.

## Sanskrit/technical terms introduced
- Moola Trikona — the "office" sign of a planet, the strongest non-exaltation seat.
- Pati — ruler/lord of a sign.
- Uccha — exaltation.
- Neecha — debilitation.
- Graha — planet.

## Names / references mentioned
- BPHS (Brihat Parashara Hora Shastra), chapter 3 verses 55-58.

# Deck 10: Solar System

**Source file:** `10. Solar System.txt`
**Type:** Astronomy fundamentals

## Topic
Tour of the Sun, Moon, and the visible planets used in Vedic astrology, with physical specs, Sanskrit names, and short descriptive notes. Closes with a scale-of-the-cosmos illustration.

## Key concepts (per body)
- **Sun (Surya / Ravi)**: 1,391,000 km diameter; revolution 225-250 million yrs around galactic center; rotation 25-35 days (differential); 333,000× Earth mass; 99.86% of solar system mass; orbits a black hole; centre of solar system; Earth orbits in 365 days. Formed 4.6 billion years ago, ~150m km from Earth, 190× Earth's circumference.
- **Moon (Chandra)**: 3,474 km; revolution & rotation both 27.3 Earth days (tidally locked); 0.0123× Earth mass; reflects Sun's light; appears same size as Sun; closest to Earth; governs tides; white; soft, pleasant, pleasing; 28 days around Earth.
- **Mars (Mangala / Kuja / Angaka)**: 6,779 km; 687 Earth days revolution; 24.6 hr rotation; 0.107× Earth mass; 2 moons (captured); no atmosphere; numerous volcanoes; red; largest dust storms; flanked by Mercury and Venus.
- **Mercury (Budha)**: 4,880 km; 88 Earth days revolution; 59 Earth days rotation; 0.055× Earth mass; 0 moons; closest to Sun; fastest orbit; tiniest planet; multi-coloured greenish-greyish.
- **Jupiter (Guru / Brihaspati)**: 139,820 km; 11.86 Earth years revolution; 9.9 hr rotation; 318× Earth mass; 79 moons; largest planet; gas; pulls Sun toward it; bulges in centre; yellow; strongest magnetic field.
- **Venus (Shukra)**: 12,104 km; 225 Earth days revolution; 243 Earth days rotation (retrograde rotation); 0.815× Earth mass; 2nd brightest object at night; hottest planet; 80% smooth; no seasons, same temperature; slowest rotation; thick atmosphere.
- **Saturn (Shani)**: 116,460 km; 29.46 Earth years revolution; 10.7 hr rotation; 95× Earth mass; 83 moons; furthest planet from Sun (in this listing); slowest moving; large but not dense; darkest planet.

## Cosmic scale exercise
- If Moon = grain of sand, Earth = pinhead 2 cm away, Sun = pool ball 6 m away, Alpha Centauri = another pool ball 1500 km away, Milky Way = size of Mercury's orbit
- 4000 stars visible on a clear night; Milky Way has 400 billion stars (counting 1/sec = 12-13,000 years)
- If Milky Way = grain of sand, known universe = 4 football fields, 100+ billion galaxies

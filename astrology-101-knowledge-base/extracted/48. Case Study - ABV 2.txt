When Vajpayee became the Prime Minister of India, he made the strategic decision to go
ahead with nuclear tests, which had been on the table for years but had been delayed due to
international pressure.

In May 1998, India conducted five nuclear tests under Operation Shakti in Pokhran, Rajasthan.
These tests were significant because they marked India's declaration as a nuclear weapons
state. The decision was controversial and drew sharp reactions internationally, leading to
sanctions from countries like the United States and Japan. Vajpayee justified the tests by
pointing to India's security needs, particularly in light of the nuclear capabilities of
neighbouring countries, China and Pakistan. The tests were seen as a way to ensure India's
strategic autonomy and security in a volatile region.

Despite the initial international backlash, including economic sanctions, the decision
eventually led to a nuanced shift in global geopolitics. Over time, relations between India and
major powers, including the U.S., improved, with India gaining greater acceptance as a
nuclear state. Vajpayee's decision significantly impacted India's strategic positioning on the
global stage.

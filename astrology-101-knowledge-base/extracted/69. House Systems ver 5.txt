Sripathi Paddhathi System
Ascendant (Lagna) - Intersection of the ecliptic with the horizon in the East
Midheaven (MC / 10th cusp) - Intersection of the ecliptic with the local meridian (longitude)


The horizon and the longitude intersect the ecliptic at different angles


The Ecliptic is tilted 23.4° relative to the celestial equator


When you move away from the Equator,
The angle between the ecliptic and the horizon changes,
The MC shifts relative to the Ascendant.
Sripathi Paddhathi System
The degree that is ascending = Mid point of the 1st House (Cusp of the 1st House)
Ascendant + 180 degrees = Descendant, i.e. cusp of the 7th House


Determine the Medium Coeli viz. the highest point on the Ecliptic. This is the cusp of the 10th House
Use https://www.onlinejyotish.com/free-astrology/vedic-horoscope.php
(navigate to Planetary Positions to find the Bhava Details)


Take difference between mid points of Ascendant and M.C. Divide by 3, to get cusps of 11th and 12th Houses
Take difference between M.C. and Descendant. Divide by 3, to get the cusps of 8th and 9th Houses


10th cusp + 180 degrees = Innum Coeli, i.e. cusp of the 4th House
Add 180 degrees to each cusp to get the opposite cusp

-   Note the longitude of the 10th House cusp from https://www.onlinejyotish.com/free-astrology/vedic-horoscope.php
-   Calculate the cusps of the 10th, 11th and 12th Houses
-   Add 1800 to the Ascendant to get the longitude of the descendant
-   Calculate the cusps of the 8th and 9th Houses
-   Add 1800 to the cusps of 8, 9, 10, 11 and 12 to get the cusps of 2, 3, 4, 5 and 6
-   Check which Graha is in which House as per the Sripathi Paddhathi method

Your sky at birth

Long before there was any software, long before there was
paper and pen, people actually looked up at the sky to locate
the navagrahas

Let’s do that..

Stand where you were born (or as close as possible), and find
the exact points in the sky where each of the navagrahas were
at your moment of birth (hint: use altitude and azimuth)

Turn and look toward each one, and see if you can connect to it

This may help build a real connection with the graha, and it lays
the foundation for understanding transits mentally, spatially
and energetically.

Your sky at birth

1. Get your birth details (date, time and place of birth)

2. Open a sky app (e.g. Stellarium), set the birth date, time and place

3. Find each of the Navagrahas using the “search” function

4. Click on the Graha, and note down it’s Azimuth and Altitude

5. Step outside. Use a compass app to face the right azimuth for
   each planet. Use your hand to guesstimate the altitude for each
   graha.

6. For each planet, face that spot in the sky. Be still for a few
   seconds, look and feel. Just notice.. Become aware that this was
   where that graha was, when you were born...

7. Write a few lines about how it felt.. Was there any planet that felt
   stronger or more familiar?

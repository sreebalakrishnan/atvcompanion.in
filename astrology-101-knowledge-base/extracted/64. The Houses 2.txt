12 Matchboxes


Get 12 matchboxes, put away the matches.

Label each matchboxes with numbers, to help you track
each House.

As we explore each House (in subsequent slides), look
around and find small objects that symbolise each House

It should be tiny, tiny, tiny..

Put it into the matchbox

Find objects that are symbolic, hold personal significance,
items that will help you connect with that House. You may
put more than one object inside each match box.

When we meet, we’ll explore these boxes in more detail.

1H: Tanu Bhava (Kendra, Trikona, Dharma)                                                                      Sun

BPHS: physique, appearance, intellect, complexion, vigour and weakness, happiness and grief, innate
nature

Brihat Jataka: determinative strength

Uttara Kalamrita: body, limbs, happiness and misery, old age, knowledge, birth place, fame, dream,
strength, dignity, politics, longevity, tranquility, age, hair, appearance, pride, livelihood, gambling for
others, stigma, honor, skin, sleep, proficiency, embezzlement, nature to insult others, freedom from
sickness, detachment, nature, agency, perseverance in cattle breeding, loss of sense of decorum,
blame from one's own castemen.

Sarvartha Chintamani: health, honour, qualities, nature, conduct, age, state, caste, purity, happiness,
misery, expression, form, colour, wife of nephew - all these are to be judged from the first house.

Hora Sara: physical growth, possessing limbs or otherwise
2H: Kutumbha Bhava (Maraka, Artha, Panapara)                                                                   Jupiter

BPHS: wealth, grains, family, death, enemies, metals, precious stones etc.

Uttara Kalamrita: speech, wealth, faith in sacred tradition, supporting others, nails, enjoyment, truth
and falsehood, tongue, eyes, clothes, diamond, copper, gems, pearl, determination, incense, family
relationship, trade, softness of speech, liberality, effort to acquire wealth, friendship, friend, splendor,
miserliness in spending, pleasant oratorical ability, scholarship, gold, silver, corn, modesty, setting
nose, firmness of mind, a close dependent, cycle of life and death, living power, finance

Sarvartha Chintamani: manikya (ruby), pearl, gem, metallic ores, articles, dress, gold, house, silver,
grains, transactions, buying and selling

Steve Jobs, Napoleon Bonaparte and Nelson Mandela find
themselves at a bar in Heaven. They pause at the entrance,
surprised that it’s super crowded

“Strange,” Napoleon thinks “We’re in Kali Yuga. I expected more
people downstairs, not here.”

“Must be a glitch in the Matrix,” Steve smirks. “Anyway, come,
let’s find a seat.”

They make their way through the crowd, passing Gandhi,
Leonardo da Vinci, Nikola Tesla, George Washington and
Winston Churchill.

Behind the bar is Varaha, polishing glasses and His tusks at the
same time. Varaha asks “What do you fellows want”

Napoleon: “Your finest creation Sir Boar. No limits. Impress me.”

Mandela raises an eyebrow. “You’re brave for a first-timer Napi.
You have no clue what He’ll put in that.” He checks out the
room. “Most folks are sipping a dark blue drink. I’ll go with that.
Tried-and-tested I guess.”

Steve: “I’ll take both. The blue drink and Napoleon’s experiment.
Let’s see what you got!”

They settle in, and scan the room.. And to their surprise they..
uhhh.. find only men

Steve: “Where have all the good women gone? Did God decide
Heaven should be men only? Another glitch - who’s doing the
QC here! I was hoping to score tonight...” He then adds “Starting
a relationship up here would be like launching a product in a
brand-new market. You’ve got to understand celestial UX/UI”

Napoleon: “Steve, love is a conquest. Strategy, decisive moves
and timing - that’s how you win. Think what I did for Josephine.
Grand gestures my mate.”

Mandela: “Ah, but grand gestures without inner strength are
fleeting. Love is not about the next big move. It’s about staying
rooted and weathering storms together.”

Steve: “Sure Mandela, but if you don’t update the system, the
software gets stale. Women, like markets, evolve. You need to
think different my friend.”

Napoleon: “All the updates in the world won’t help if you blow
the first impression. One bold entrance can define everything.
Josephine knew from the first letter I wrote to her”

Mandela: “Exactly, and those letters were too much, too soon.
Love unfolds. You don’t lead someone into it, you walk with
them.”

Napoleon stiffens, quite offended.

Steve grins, bouncing his leg. “Maybe it’s both. Innovation and
steadiness. Initiative and listening. Love’s like a good OS, part
hardware, part software. Needs updates and uptime. But where
are the women?”

Varaha arrives with the drinks, and places them on the counter
with a grunt.

“You blokes talk a lot about women. But you haven’t noticed the
obvious, have you? Gentlemen. This is Our Gay Bar. And
Tuesdays are men-only. Jeez, I wonder what Duality and
Modality each of you are.”

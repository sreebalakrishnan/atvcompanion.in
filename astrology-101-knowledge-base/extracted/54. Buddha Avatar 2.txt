His teaching
1. Life is full of suffering
2. Attachment is the root cause of suffering
3. Let go of attachment, you will let go of suffering
4. To let go of attachment, follow the Eightfold Noble Path

Wisdom, Prajna
• Right View (understanding the truths of life)
• Right Intention (free of ill-will and harmful desires)

Ethics, Sila
• Right Speech (truthful, kind and helpful communication)
• Right Action (moral and peaceful behaviour)
• Right Livelihood (earning a living without harming others)

Discipline, Samadhi
• Right Effort (cultivating positive mental states)
• Right Mindfulness (awareness of body, feelings and thoughts)
• Right Concentration (deep focus through meditation)
                                                                 Shakyamuni Buddha

A!rologically
Intelligence
Am I searching for an academic and intellectual understanding,
or for wisdom that transforms me?

Communication
How effective is my communication? Is this my strength or
weakness? Do my words bring clarity and positivity, or do they
cause confusion or harm?

The Middle Path
Do I live a balanced life? Do I go to extremes, or do I try for
balance in my thoughts, words and deeds?

Equanimity and detachment
Am I attached to anything? What am I attached to in life? How
can I simplify this, and focus on what truly matters?

Staying balanced
Do I stay calm and cool when making decisions, or does my
emotions cloud my judgment?                                       Shakyamuni Buddha

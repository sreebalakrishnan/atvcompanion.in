Astronomically

Bhudevi sinking into the cosmic ocean may refer to all
of creation being pulled into a black hole. Black holes,
like the asuras, consume everything, including light and
order, they may represent chaos, greed and an
unsatiable appetite.

Varaha dives into this and lifts creation back up,
becomes a symbol of something that rescues light from
darkness.

Maybe Black Holes can never truly destroy creation? It
may be hidden, but it can be recovered. So maybe
Varaha is a “white hole”, the opposite of black holes,
where matter and information re-emerge, symbolizing
that everything can return even from total collapse?

Philosophically

Hiranyaksha was egoistic, and obsessed with greed and
power. If so, then Bhudevi symbolises Truth and Dharma
which are hijacked by ego, pride and power and taken
to the “ocean” of tamas (darkness, inertia and
confusion). Dharma awaits to be rescued through deep,
inner effort.

Varaha is an unexpected, primal drive of the Divine to
go where our ordinary minds cannot. It dives into the
unconscious, unafraid of darkness, and rescues
Dharma. This is the essence of sadhana - the courage to
go deep, and retrieve what is sacred, and bring it back
to awareness.

Self reflection

Bhudevi was taken to the depths of the ocean. Similarly,
sometimes parts of us get buried deep under layers of
emotion, and it takes courage to bring them back to
light. What part of you feels like it has sunk into chaos
or confusion? How can you go deep to retrieve it?

Varaha emerged in a wild, unexpected form - half-boar,
half-god. He challenged conventional ideas of “God”
and showed that power can arise from unlikely shapes.
Have you ever been misunderstood or treated like an
outsider because of a path you chose?

Self reflection

Hiranyaksha believed he had won, but it was an illusion
that could not last. When have you mistaken illusion
for truth? Or chased something that turned out to be
empty in the end?

Hiranyaksha’s drive for power made him sink the Earth
itself, showing how desires can lead to destruction
rather than fulfilment. Do you have any desires that is
making you sink to the bottom?

The cosmic ocean may be symbolic of the mind, so
large that it seems to swallow the whole Earth. Are
you amplifying anything out of proportion?

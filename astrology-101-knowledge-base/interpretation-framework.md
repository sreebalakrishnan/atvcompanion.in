# Interpretation Framework — Vikram's two anchors

**Source:** Deck 72 (Sun) · Vikram's Astrology 101 class · pedagogical pattern shared in his own teaching
**Use:** Canonical reference for any surface in the project that scaffolds a learner writing their own chart interpretation. Currently surfaced inline in `read.html` (above the per-house exercise textarea) and as a self-check below it.

---

## Anchor 1 — Parts of Speech

A grammar-as-interpretation map. Vikram teaches that any chart placement can be read as a sentence, with each grammatical role mapping to a chart factor.

| Grammar | Chart factor | What it does | Example for Sun in Aries 2H |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Noun** | **Planet** | What is acting — the core energy or function being interpreted | Sun · self |
| **Verb** | **Duality** (Masculine / Feminine) | The direction of expression — outward or inward, active or receptive | Aries · yang · outward |
| **Adverb** | **Modality** (Cardinal / Fixed / Mutable) | How the action happens — initiates, sustains, or changes | Aries · cardinal · leads |
| **Adjective** | **Element** (Fire / Earth / Air / Water) | The kind / quality of the action — fiery, practical, intellectual, emotional | Aries · Fire · warmth |
| **Area of life** | **House** | Where it shows up — the area of life being affected | 2H · family |

**Worked example — Sun in Aries 2H:**

> *"The self expresses outwardly by leading warmly the family"*
>
> or
>
> *"The self shines by leading the family with warmth"*

The grammar pattern means a learner can build a coherent sentence for any planet × sign × house combination by walking through the five roles in order — and the same factual placement yields different surface sentences depending on word choice, which is exactly the room for the learner's voice that Vikram protects.

## Anchor 2 — The Intention

Four framings the same fact can land in. Year-1 of the class uses three; one is explicitly excluded.

| Framing | Voice | Example (for Sun in Aries 2H) |
|---|---|---|
| ~~To predict the future~~ | declarative future | ~~"You will be confident in public speaking"~~ · ~~"You are a public speaker"~~ |
| **To initiate a conversation** | open question | "Have you ever spoken to an audience?" |
| **To invite a course of action** | offered invitation | "Can you try presenting what you know, and see how you feel?" |
| **To remedy the planet** | suggested remedy | "Consider presenting what you know" |

**The principle:** the chart is not a forecast tool. Prediction-framing closes possibility; the other three open it. A skilled reader (the cohort, eventually) chooses the framing based on what the moment needs — never landing in prediction.

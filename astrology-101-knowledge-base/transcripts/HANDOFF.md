### Sripathi Paddhati — How to Calculate Houses

**Core idea:** Accounts for the fact that the ecliptic cuts the horizon at an angle that changes with latitude. Further from the equator = more unequal houses. At the equator, Sripathi gives the same result as Equal House.

**Guiding sutra:** *"Planets closer to the midpoint of their house give stronger results in that area of life."*

#### The 4 Anchor Points

| Reference Point | House Midpoint | How to Get It |
|---|---|---|
| Ascendant (ASC) | 1st house | Degree rising on eastern horizon at birth |
| Descendant (DSC) | 7th house | ASC + 180° |
| Medium Coeli (MC) | 10th house | Look up from ephemeris/software — NOT ASC + 90° |
| Imum Coeli (IC) | 4th house | MC + 180° |

> At the equator, MC = Zenith = exactly 90° from ASC. As you move north/south, MC drifts from Zenith, so the arc from MC to ASC may be 87°, 93°, etc.

#### Step-by-Step

**Step 1:** Get ASC and MC from birth chart software.
*Example: ASC = 322° (22° Aquarius), MC = 235° (25° Scorpio)*

**Step 2:** Calculate MC-to-ASC arc:
> Arc = ASC − MC = 322 − 235 = **87°** (spans houses 10, 11, 12)

**Step 3:** Trisect the arc:
> Each house = 87 ÷ 3 = **29°**

**Step 4:** Mark midpoints of houses 11 and 12 by stepping back from ASC:
- 12th house midpoint = 322 − 29 = **293°** (23° Capricorn)
- 11th house midpoint = 293 − 29 = **264°** (24° Sagittarius)
- 10th house midpoint = already known (MC = 235°)

**Step 5:** House boundaries = average of two adjacent midpoints
> E.g., boundary between 11th and 12th = (264 + 293) ÷ 2 = 278.5°

**Step 6:** Lower half (houses 4, 5, 6):
- IC = MC + 180° → 4th house midpoint
- DSC = ASC + 180° → 7th house midpoint
- Trisect IC-to-DSC arc → 5th and 6th house midpoints

**Step 7:** Houses 2, 3, 8, 9: add 180° to their opposites.

**When used in course:** Year 4+. Full Sign system used for years 1–3+.

### Equal House System — How to Calculate Houses

**Core idea:** The Ascendant degree is the **midpoint** of the 1st house. Every house is exactly **30°** wide (15° on either side of its midpoint). Only the ASC is needed — no MC lookup required.

**Guiding sutra:** Same as Sripathi — planets near the midpoint give stronger results.

#### One Rule

> **ASC degree = midpoint of the 1st house**
> Each next house midpoint = previous + 30°
> House boundary = midpoint ± 15°

#### Critical: Two Charts Required

Unlike Full Sign (where Rashi chart = Bhava chart), Equal House requires **two separate charts**:
- **Rashi chart** — which *sign* each planet is in
- **Bhava chart** — which *house* each planet is in

*Example from class:* Moon in Libra + ASC 22° Aquarius → Moon is in the **9th house** in the Bhava chart. You cannot read houses from the Rashi chart or signs from the Bhava chart.

### Three Systems Compared

|   | Full Sign | Equal House | Sripathi Paddhati |
|---|---|---|---|
| House size | Always 30° | Always 30° | Varies by latitude |
| ASC position | Anywhere in the sign | Always the midpoint | Always the midpoint |
| MC needed? | No | No | Yes (from software) |
| Rashi = Bhava? | Yes | No | No |
| When used in course | Years 1–3+ | Study only | Year 4+ |

## Key Concepts (from handoff quick-reference)

- **Vikram's teaching approach:** mythology first → astronomy → astrology. Stories encode planetary archetypes; astronomy provides mathematical precision; astrology synthesises both.
- **Grounding:** every session begins with breathwork (box breathing, alternate nostril, etc.)
- **Swadharma vs Swabhava:** your duty/path vs your true nature — alignment between them = no inner conflict.
- **Uttarayana / Dakshinayana:** sun's northward (spring/summer) and southward (autumn/winter) journeys.
- **Kala Purusha:** cosmic person — body parts mapped to zodiac signs (Aries=head → Pisces=feet), used in medical astrology.
- **Purusharthas:** 4 aims of life mapped to house groups — Dharma (1,5,9), Artha (2,6,10), Kama (3,7,11), Moksha (4,8,12).
- **Ayanamsha:** difference between Western tropical zodiac and Vedic sidereal zodiac (accounts for precession).
- **Rahu/Ketu:** lunar nodes — points where moon's orbit intersects the ecliptic; karmic significance.
- **Eclipse conditions:** (1) Sun & Moon on the same axis AND (2) their declinations match Rahu/Ketu.
- **Medium Coeli (MC):** where the ecliptic intersects the local meridian; midpoint of 10th house.
- **Zenith:** point directly overhead; equals MC only at the equator.
- **Panchang:** Indian lunar calendar — 12 masas (months) grouped into 6 ritus (seasons).

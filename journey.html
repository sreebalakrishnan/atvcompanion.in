A new way to learn

A workbook-team framework, made tangible. Each topic unfolds in four layers — Watch the class, Revise what was covered, Reflect on one prompt, Integrate by bringing it into your week. The next topic only unlocks once you've recorded your integration of this one.

workbook team · pedagogy vikram has chosen

Four layers per topic — in sequence

The framework the workbook team is building the curriculum around — initially proposed by Marie, now being shaped by the whole team. This is the architecture Vikram has chosen for the workbook, and we're testing whether the same shape works for the companion app. A learner doesn't come to this page to discover material for the first time. They've already attended the class, or watched the video. This page is where the integration happens — after the live experience, not instead of it. The sequence matters; the timing of unveiling is the pedagogy.

  • WatchAttended live, or caught up by video. Without this, the rest is out of context.
  • ReviseQuiz-style recall. No new material — just jogging the memory.
  • ReflectOne prompt. Sit with it. Write what arises.
  • IntegrateBring it into your week. Record what you did, where you caught it, what shifted.

locked

Three integration methods are shown below — for Wednesday's team review. Each is a different posture on the question Vikram raised: "how do we know they have done it?"

Lightest touch — the learner writes what they did. We trust the recording.

Diet-app pattern — the learner uploads a photograph as evidence (a journal page, an object that triggered the integration, themselves in the moment).

Auroville pattern — the integration is reviewed by a peer buddy. Generates a shareable link.

# User Testing — Astrology 101 Companion App

A short guide for the first cohort of learners trying the page. Authored by Sree & Sanchi after Vikram's review on 7 May 2026.

## Why this matters

So far the page has been built in an echo chamber — Sree, Sanchi, occasionally Sudha. Vikram blessed continuing the build, *but* explicitly asked for structured user feedback before we go wider. Without it we're guessing at what helps the learner.

This document holds the cohort, the questions, the synthesis cadence. Sanchi owns the loop.

## The cohort

Aim for **5 learners** for the first round. Mix of:

- Someone already using it actively (Sudha — power user)
- Someone using it lightly (Anil, Kajal — early triers)
- Someone newer to the class who hasn't seen the page yet (TBD — Sanchi to nominate)
- Someone older / less device-comfortable (Hiran or Meera — important for accessibility signal)
- Someone Vikram suggests

Each person gets **2 weeks** with the page. Open-ended use, not prescribed exercises. The goal is to see whether the page draws them back without nudging.

## How we ask

Send via WhatsApp:

> *"Sree & Sanchi — we've been building a study companion for Vikram's class. Here it is: [link]. Could you spend 2 weeks with it (on phone or laptop, whatever you use), and then send back voice notes or written answers to the seven questions below? No right answers — we want your honest experience."*

## The seven questions

1. **What did you find difficult?** Anything confusing, hard to find, hard to read, or that didn't work the way you expected.
2. **What did you learn that surprised you?** Anything the page made click for you that the class hadn't yet.
3. **What did you expect that wasn't there?** A feature, a topic, a kind of help you went looking for and didn't find.
4. **Would you come back to this in your study week? Yes / no, and why?** Honest read on whether the page earns a place in your routine.
5. **What would bring you back?** If yes — what makes you return. If no — what would change that.
6. **Did anything feel out of place?** Wording that didn't sound like Vikram, design that felt off, things that didn't fit the class's voice.
7. **On a scale of 1-10, would you pay ₹500/month for this once it's polished?** Commercial signal. Not yet a real ask.

## Format

Voice notes are fine. Written is fine. Form is fine. We adapt to the learner. If they only answer 3 questions, that's still useful.

Sanchi's role: light structuring on the way in (so people understand the seven questions), full structuring on the way out (synthesise themes weekly).

## What we're listening for

Three signal types:

**Stop signals.** Someone explicitly says they won't come back, or finds the page actively confusing in a way that suggests we got the model wrong. These are urgent — pause, fix, re-test.

**Resonance signals.** Someone uses a phrase Vikram used in class without prompting. Someone reads the cabinet story aloud. Someone references a section without thinking. These tell us the voice is landing.

**Friction signals.** Someone stumbles over font size, button placement, language choices. These are the easy wins — fix and move on.

## Synthesis cadence

- **Weekly:** Sanchi reviews the responses received, identifies 1-2 themes, writes 5 lines of summary in this doc (append below).
- **Monthly:** A synthesis sent to Vikram. One paragraph, three bullets. Not weekly chatter — he gets the punchline.
- **Decision moments:** When themes are clear, Sanchi flags them in a 30-min Sree sync. We pick what to address in the next ship.

## Pricing — what we're really asking with Q7

We're not asking *will you pay this much?* We're asking *do you value this enough that the question makes sense?* Anyone who answers >5 is signal that this could be a real product. Anyone <5 is signal that the page needs more value before we go paid. No one is being charged today.

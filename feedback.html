how to give us feedback · v4.74.3

Feedback is a gift

Good, bad, ugly, to-improve — bring it on. The next few months of this app get built with you, not at you. Whatever resonated, whatever felt like dissonance — we want to hear it.

"Bring it on. Don't hesitate. The good, the bad, the ugly, the to-improve — please bring it on."

— the invitation, in full

the invitation

Just speak up — no judgment

You're a text person? You found the long sections too heavy. You're a visual learner? The wall of words lost you. A section made your stomach knot. Something else made you lean forward. All of it matters. The page only gets better if we know what's working and what isn't.

You don't need to phrase it diplomatically. You don't need to know why something didn't land — only that it didn't. We'll figure out the why together.

  • This section was too text-heavy — I switched off halfway.
  • I'm a visual learner. Where are the pictures?
  • The Cabinet story confused me. I couldn't tell which planet exalts where.
  • I love the interactive parts — give me more of those.
  • I felt overwhelmed when I opened the Houses section.
  • The aspects topic is something I struggle with.

Those are real examples. Every one of them led to a release — sometimes within hours.

what it looks like in practice

Ayesha asked — and the Aspects page was born

11 May 2026 · today

From a voice note to a shipped page in a few hours

Ayesha — one of the cohort in the current batch, from Australia — sent a voice note this morning. Two things in it: a flag that the Cabinet's exaltation paragraph felt confusing around Cancer, and a passing line that aspects (Mars 4/8, Jupiter 5/9, Saturn 3/10) are something she struggles to remember.

By lunchtime, the Cabinet paragraph was split into four per-planet scene cards. By the afternoon, a new Aspects · drishti page had shipped — pick a planet, tap a sign, watch the gaze light up. Her name's on the footer. Her ask, her page.

That's not unusual. That's the loop. Someone says "I'd love to learn this differently." We build it. They reply, we iterate. Sometimes a release lands in hours; sometimes in a week. But it lands.

who this is for

The cohort — and anyone who loves a Socratic answer

If you're studying with Vikram — in 101, in 102, or as a continuing student who's been at it for years — this page is for you. So is the rest of the app. We're building for the twenty-to-fifty learners actively in the rooms, plus the seasoned practitioners passionate enough to want a new way to learn, reflect, and test their own memory.

Vikram teaches by the Socratic method — there's no right or wrong answer in a class. The same goes here. If a section makes you go "hmm, that's not how I'd phrase it" — that's feedback. If a quiz answer feels too rigid — that's feedback. If a topic was covered in class and you can't find it here — that's feedback.

the easiest channel

WhatsApp us — voice notes especially welcome

One number. Type, voice-note, photograph the screen with arrows drawn on it — whatever's fastest for you. There's no form to fill in, no fields to mis-format. Just say what you want to say.

after you send

What happens next

Feedback doesn't fall into a void. Here's the actual loop:

  1. You send — a voice note, a screenshot, a one-liner. Whatever you have time for.
  2. Sree's assistant listens — consolidates the ask with anything else that's similar, and queues it for the next build pass.
  3. We build — most fixes land within hours or days. Bigger things take a week or two. Anything outside our skill stays on the page as "asked for, deferred."
  4. You'll know — either a direct message back saying it's live, or you'll see it credited in the release notes (those are quite detailed — your name will be on the entry).

Today alone — three releases shipped because of one cohort member's voice note this morning. That's not a special case. That's the rhythm.
